10 [31fbpnust8bpu] January 28, 2009 at 06:35PM
The scanning is the tedious part. No sure how an app on the sever side would make that easier. I unstapled these, then scanned them as legal-sized double-sided through my auto-sheet feeder. That makes the scanning go pretty quickly. Then I used IrfanView in batch mode to crop the left and right sides of each scan to give an individual file for each page. Then I re-assembled the individual images in to a multi-page TIF, and printed that as a PDF. The tricky part is getting IrfanView to name the individual page files to express the correct page order so that you can easily combine them in their proper sequence for viewing in a PDF.

Morgan (Susan McDow) gave me some of her B&M stash at the Catherders' Tilden potlach last year to scan for her. I've slowly and sporadically rendered two so far in PDF format. Both are from the '90s, dates of issue in the filenames. They aren't small--particularly the issue from '94--as various image compression algorithms available in the PDF envelope can seriously fuzz-out faint grayscale text unless high dpi/low-compressions are used---at least in my OEM/Public Domain PDF utils. Sue has asked me to go ahead and post these here on the Teahouse site. Catt suggested this forum thread... Debi mentions her upload as a 'test'. Perhaps B&M could have it's own Page, Catt? or a group? Not sure how things are stuctured here, so if there's a better place as time goes by let me know and I'll re-submit. Thank you so much for posting that. Without a doubt, that was one of the most informative reads I've had in quite a long time, particularly from a Faire perpsective. Like Sylvia, that came out toward the end of my Faire/Fair tenure (I quit after RPFN 1979). I realize I'd always had a very bad taste in my mouth about RPFN 1978 (which is allued to in the B&M), and really only did my final RPFN 1979 stint because of affection for Luisa (whom I hold very dear) who was trying to get up and running as QE1 on a full time basis. She needed solidarity in an existing, experienced Court and not a bunch of newbies, so a lot of us stuck it out to provide a degree of continuity. Of course she was absolutely brilliant (as her subsequent years showed), but if it were not for her, I would have left after for good after RPFN 1978. I don't regret staying, but after reading this B&M (I never read them at the time--there was such a rift between the actors and the crew and the vendors I didn't even know it existed until years later), I see in print what others must have been feeling, too. RPFN 1978 marked the end of an era. I was so put out I didn't even both to work DCF in 1978, although for years I convinced myself I had. Why not, right? On a personal note, my folks were splitting up at the time, and I didn't need my fantasy land/escape to mirror what was going on at home as well. I'd love to read Vol. 1, Issues 1 & 2 if they ever surface. Thanks again!
