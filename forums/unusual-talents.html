It's amazing how many people I meet who are "public speakers" or "performers" and can't function w/o a mic, especially if the audience is more than say, 10 people.

19 [126ln5dg00p4l] September 04, 2009 at 06:42PM
In addition to the packing-for-a-weekend skills, over the years working on Faire stages I learned to create an entertaining show on a bare stage, out of whatever was at hand. We had our own costumes and a few props for the shows, but it was essentially a bare stage, and when we moved around from Lydian Grove to the Ale Garden we learned to make use of whatever 'set' there was. When I went to work for the little community theatre across the street from me, I wrote and directed a few shows---and the staff and crew there had serious doubts: but it was nothing I hadn't been doing for decades. I used my old Faire-learned skills and it all came together beautifully. At the Faire and in front of a live (sometimes "raw') audience you can see in full daylight you understand that its the characters and the story and the style that make it work. If you can make them want to sit still for twenty minutes amid all the noise and distraction, you've learned something. No big costumes, no flashy lighting design, no moving sets or special effects. Just people.
