8 PatriciaBlanco November 18, 2009 at 05:34PM
Other 'Mime' persons at the Faire (limited list, as I did not begin 'Faire-ing' until S'72): Robert Shields (from ? to possibly N'71-ish) Tommy McLaughlin (S'72 - S'74-ish) Tina Lenert (S'72 - S'80-ish) - Tina was th\e delightful 'Doll Toy' who would interact with audience in the streets of Agoura Faire. She rarely played Northern Faires, and while I cannot recall the last Faire she worked 'full-time' as a street mime, I do have fond memories of her doing at least one day at S'80-ish (she may have been gate-listed for the occasion), when she did a short mime routine on Maybower Stage during the Queen's Show. Since she and I were long-time friends from our mutual miming days in the 70's, that occasion at Faire may have been a one-day only showing for me, since that was my first return to Southern Faire and start of my 'long run' as QER-I. Mitchell Young Evans (now known as Mitchell Evans) - who succeeded to being The Mime of the Faire after Tommy McLaughlin gave up the role in 75-ish. Interesting (I hope) Faire trivia: long before starting Faires, I knew Mitchell from our college drama department, where he was known more as a musician, singer, actor, and all around bon vivant. We both got into Pantomime there, and and then started studying same with Richmond Sheppard ('America's Foremost Mime') in Hollywood. One day in April 1972, out of the blue, I get a call from Mitchell to hurry over to a place off Laurel Canyon in the hills and that I should wear 'mime' attire (make-up optional, due to the late notice). It seems that Mitchell had landed a last-minute audition spot with Donna Larson for the upcoming RPFS72. Well, I ran to grab my tights and tunic and Donna graciously auditioned us, even though she told us that between belly dancers and mimes, she had just about seen everything that could be done with those ('except a white-faced belly dancer,' she laughed, and hastily told us that she was*NOT* requesting that). Well, long story shortened: Mitchell, myself, and a third mime by the name of Katee McClouer (sp?), got the passes to do some stage and street mime at Faire that spring. Two food tickets each and the permission to pass-the-hat after our street (but *not* stage) act. So that is how I came to Faire: via the Mime Route. Well, that was a long-long time ago. However, I can say (with some pride, I suppose) that despite the passage of time, even today I can still fit into my white face. ;-P
