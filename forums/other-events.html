11 PatriciaBlanco May 26, 2009 at 08:46PM
Hi all, Luisa here: I was at Shindig, playing 'Miss Russmore,' the school marm (a completely fabricated character). My gigs included lecturing at the Chitaquaw Tent, narrating "The Midnight Ride of Paul Revere" with Brian Patterson miming all the action alluded to in the poem, and doing a bunch of audience participation gigs in and around the Shindig site. I also got to play a minor role in Dale Hill's "Richard III" (a truncated Shakespeare production that was thrown together by 'Edward Boothe' (Dale) and his company with the purpose of earning just enough money to get the heck out of the tiny town of Blackpoint). The entire production was a delibrate farce, and a lot of fun to do. If I recall correctly, Michael Levine (aka Cookie Jester) played the part of Wild Bill Hickoc (sp?) while hawking from a platform above his catering booth that featured buffalo burgers, buffalo chili, and other such 'western themed' foods. His costume was outrageous, huge white leather rodeo parade chaps, gawdy vest, and a cowboy hat that would have shaded a family of ten, at least. Most memorable, though, was his astonishing long, white haired wig and possibly a huge handlebar mustashe as well - really can't remember the facial hair, though: could not get past the halarious wig! God he was funny! Not always in period (Michael was infameously wicked in that way), but always a real scream. Sigh ... I miss his genuine spit-fire wit. The man was absolutely amazing. Hmmm ... other characters: Brett Kume (sp?) with Stuart White and others as the Blackpoint Mud Hens, the local baseball team (*great* period team uniforms, and a halarious (okay, no spell check, sue me ;-P)team song that I wish I had an audio for. Sigh ... another great memory. Karana Drayton and her husband Michael, hosted up a genuine fiddling competition that drew outstanding fiddlers from all over California (Michael, himself, was a past top fiddling champion from the same circuit that these others came from - of course they all knew each other, don't we all?). That was held on an outdoor stage between the Red Barn and the parking lot. I *think* the success of that competition lead to the later Blackpoint Music Festival, but I can't document that, alas. BTW: Blackpoint Music Festival dates: it definitely was later than Shindig, but not in the 1980's at all. I was elsewhere during the summers of 77 - 79, so all I know is that it was one of those summers. Back to Shindig: Janet Bigglestone Winters (she was still Bigglestone then) did a wonderful Women's Sufferage character (Elizabeth Cady Staton or Susan B. Anthony? Alas, can't recall specifically). Her bustle skirt walking dress was spot-on accurate and she handled the audience participation of signing the petition for women's sufferage in a wonderful, sincere, and gentle yet amusing way. Really, her personal best at street theater, I thought, even though some thought it a bit too 'low key.' I loved what she did. John Achorn as Emperor Norton, Jon Decles/Don Studebaker as Mark Twain, Will Wood as Rutherford B. Hayes, candidate for the US Presidency, ... ummm ... oh dear, can't remember more right now. Peck away, oh fine Historic Leagionnaires!

Yeah, the Shindig. That was my first time as a participant. We had a fur trade era re-enactment group and we were the mountain men. 16 [02v9sfrs8abuf] May 18, 2009 at 06:03PM
The thing I remember most about the Shindig was a mixture of good and bad. The good...I remember helping Carolie and Doris make my costume. It was from scratch and they showed so much patience with me...I remember sitting with them sewing on my buttons. Okay...that's about it. This was my LAST faire working as an entertainer. My memory is a bit foggy on it (hey it's been 33 years folks) but I remember it was SO disorganized that it was stupid. I was a Theatre Arts Major at San Francisco State at the time, and I was ALL about professionalism in my craft. I had a Make-up emphasis but I still LOVED acting. I was young (not even 20 yet), and probably a bit naive and VERY idealistic. I can't remember what exactly it was that broke this camels back but I do remember my mentor Dennis Day saying to me, "Kalyn...your problem is that you think this (ALL FAIRES not just this one) is a professional gig. You aim to be a professional and you have to understand this is non-professional, amateur theatre." WOW! The penny dropped for me BIG TIME. I figured because there was money exchanged, be it through food tickets or salary...both of which I received, it was a professional gig. I realized that I had lost the magic and awe and considered Faire a real gig. I WANTED that feeling of happiness and innocence back, so I quit and continued on for only food tickets in the middle of the run. Well...it didn't help unfortunately. I remember crying a lot during that faire. I might have been me and my age and mind set, but the following Faire (can't remember if it was Dickens or RPFS) I was a stage manager and hawker. Made more money, got more stuff and had MORE fun than I ever had as an actor. Eventually I went into retail with Feather. I ALWAYS had a foot in the acting field...I always did my gig as a washer woman because I loved it. Always did my bit with J Paul when he was Drake (I was Bethany Drake his twice removed neice...we did a bit at the well on his way to the Queen's Show that was pretty funny if I do say so myself), and mostly I joined him onstage for that show...one of the only peasants allowed on stage because after all...I was a Drake.
