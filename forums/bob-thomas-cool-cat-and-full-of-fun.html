9 [oq0azs9woanj] January 19, 2009 at 09:19PM
When I was a little girl, Bob was living with my dad in Fairfax and making an exquisite harp with Mary - all inlaid with shell and ebony (if I remember it correctly - I was only around 8 or 9 at the time). I used to go into his workroom and visit. He mostly told me stories of history and historical characters, but one day he told me a story that changed my world view. I had been admiring a sword that leaned against the bookshelf for many days, never touching it or asking, just admiring while he told me his stories. I think he was actually waiting for me to ask, but I never did. One day he told me it was a Spanish sword, asking me if I wanted to pick it up. I remember turning to look at him to see if the offer was genuine. He had laid down his tools and swivelled on his chair to give me his full attention. With very wide eyes, I nodded a very firm Yes. I reached with one hand, and he recommended two. That sword was nearly as tall as I was, it seemed. I reached and with two hands using every ounce of strength I hefted that sword and lifted its tip nearly to my waist before I ran out of puff and had to lower it to the floor. I clumsily replaced it back to where it had rested. I was excited, the feel of the sword and it's weight was unlike anything I had ever felt before, but had an exciting familiarity at the same time. Bob smiled and asked "Do you know the story of Queen Bodacea?" And so I was told the tale of the warrior queen. Changed my life.

4 or 5 years before Bob passed, I had hired him to play at an SCA event in Grass Valley. He just finished a set on his dudelsack and a young woman went up and asked him if he knew any Scottish music. He said yes, and went on to say that "These are Belgian bagpipes, my dear. They do not play in the same key as Scottish music." The young woman said she understood, then asked where Belgium was. Bob responded with, "It is the country just north of France." The young woman replied with, "I don't know where that is either, but that's probably because I'm from San Rafael." The look on Bob's face was priceless.

18 [2c9hwt6yphj6c] February 02, 2008 at 03:10AM
circa early 80's - lark camp - i'm in on the mullah's ticket as was bob. he could've had most any job but he preferred to wash dishes. anyway, that year jehan paul was organizing the "talent" show and i caught this brilliant flash: what a hoot as well as a great visual if bob, his rangy self dressed in a sailor suit, danced a hornpipe while i attempted to play Sailors Hornpipe (that's the popeye themesong for you uninitiated) on this trumpet that for some reason i was dragging around. to my surprise, bob immediately saw the wisdom in this as soon as i brought it up. somehow, someone came up with the suit complete with hat (not the old pancake type w/side ribbon but what the hell...) and the gig was booked. when it was our turn on that fateful night, i walked down to the apron of the stage and said something like, "the piece we'd like to perform for you tonight is, i believe, from Swan Lake", and with that launched into this raggedy-assed rendition on my horn while bob danced this amazing hornpipe complete with airborne heel clicks from one side to the other. now, i'd like to tell you we brought down the house, but i do remember lots of smiles and laughter. if you knew bob you're likely smiling right now. if for some reason you missed this performance, all is not necessarily lost - which brings me to the real point of this tale: it was captured on video by one ray griswold. now i haven't thought about it in a long time nor have i asked ray if he might be able to come up with said video, but if you see him before i do please feel free. who knows, a true treasure might just be unearthed.
