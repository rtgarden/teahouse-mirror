11 [0grgrunot1fm3] January 06, 2009 at 12:49PM
I had only one audition for any faire and it was the worst audition that I''ve ever done, before or after. And it scarred me for every after. It was Dickens, first year at Ft. Mason, I was 12 or 13 and I knew scads of dirty sea shanties from growing up on the waterfront in the Gates. I was auditioning to sing at Mad Sal's. Singing was never my strongest skill but for some reason I thought I could pull it off. As I waited my turn in my ragged, no longer white sailor middy and tattered skirt, Michael Parvin -who I did not know at the time- was audition for the V&A as Champagne Charley. He did his ENTIRE song directly and flirtatiously to me. I was nervous already from waiting and thinking I was going to sing and being 13 and alone there. I was not at all prepared for this. I went on directly after him. I was such a wreck that I destroyed the song and all the auditioners could do was argue over whether the song was period or W.W.II (it had been re-popularized during the War). Years later when I met Michael again and worked with him in New Castle, I remembered very clearly who he was. Something in my attitude towards him kept him from from doing his oily, put-the-make-on thing with me and we managed to get along fine.

14 Eric Lethe January 24, 2008 at 08:34AM
Carol La Fleur used to audition new shows during run of Faire (especially towards the end of Agoura for Blackpoint). These auditions were sometimes held near the end of the day on an otherwise closed stage such as Drury Creek. Catt has written some on Tribe about the Reduced Shakespeare Company audition at Agoura, which was done in what is now the "picnic shed" down near the Western Town (was used for carpentry and props a couple of times I think). There's actually a video of this audition...the first time we saw Hamlet condensed to 30 minutes...on their DVD (in the extra materials (and Adam Long is very funny in it). But what I remember as the single most magical audition I ever saw was at Drury Creek in the early 1980s around 6:00pm...the audition of the La Famiglia Bologna (when Marilyn Letchworth was still a Bologna)...By the time Carol actually hired them for daily stage performance, they had "lost the skirts" and were doing all-male Commedia as Il Fratelli Bologna, but that's another story... What was magical about it? Well of course it was the end of another sun-drenched Agoura day and the shadows were getting long but the light was still golden. Remember how the dust from all those feet used to settle down finally towards the end of the day to where it just gave a lustre to the air? And the air was warm but finally not hot...And the Bolognas...they were just SO GOOD. Polished. Professional compared to so many of the shows at Faire. Still raunchy humor, but also magical effects such as Marilyn appearing out of thin air (she is so small she could hide onstage under another character's cloak until it was time for her appearance). And timing. I was completely flattered when they asked me to stage manage their gigs in San Francisco; they were at the height of their game. The next year everything on Drury Creek seemed to be at that level...the "Reeve's Tale", the "Pyramus & Thisbee" with Greg Propst, even "The Sherriff Needs a Wyfe". Of all the work I did at Faire, I think that year was my favorite.
