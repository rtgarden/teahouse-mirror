An interesting note on my above reply re: box stores and their nursery suppliers. This week another nursery that supplies 3 major chains declared bankruptcy. Bordiers Nursery is/was 86 years old. Part of the problem involves the old traveling salesmen cliche.There are certain plant salesmen that approach nurseries with their hyped up "I'll send your sales through the roof" pitches. They land box store accounts by cutting the price of the plants, giving "guaranteed sales", granting exclusivity, etc. They make large commissions, drain the nursery, then leave flush, while the nursery goes belly up.They neglect the long term clients of the nursery, basically burning those bridges so badly that the scorned nurseries are very hesitant to do business when the stricken nursery tries to go back and re-establish relations. A cautionary tale for all.
2 PatriciaBlanco January 06, 2009 at 04:17AM
Yes Luisa, red worms compost newspaper well. You might want to shred it up finely to give it a head start. As for worm poop there are at least a couple of brands in wide distribution. We sell "WormGrow"($8.50 for 1 cubic foot). There is also a brand called "Worm Glo".It's widely available in nurseries and even box stores like Home depot(not a good place to buy plants). A word about box stores, You don't know where or how the plants were grown. Many corporate nurseries give a micro feeding every time they water. They can deteriorate quickly when taken off their feed. Also, ethically, they stink. The nurseries who supply those plants only get paid for each plant scanned at the register. And when the plant is returned dead(1 year guarantee) the nursery loses that money. Not the box store. The box stores are not even required to take care of the plants. The box stores also lock in exclusivity with some nurseries. I've seen nurseries all excited to land a box store account, only to go bankrupt. The local family owned nursery may seem to cost a little more, but wouldn't you rather have a well tended plant, local grown, with friendly, knowledgeable help. Like me!

4 PatriciaBlanco December 31, 2008 at 02:12AM
We are big fans of worm poop. It is a vegan friendly fertilizer(of importance to some). But I really love the systemic way it repels aphids and whitefly. A half inch thick around your plants once a year works well.The only thing is, this works only in the organic garden. Chemicals will negate the effect. I also like the use of shredded cedar mulch as a top cover. Besides other mulching benefits, it smells great and also helps in natural insect repelling. Think cedar hope chest. Of course we like some bugs. I prefer passive insect control. But I gotta say. in certain gardens, in certain applications, I say NUKE the little creeps.

Here is what I have done. My friend brought some quite well aged & innoffensive manure. It was somewhat powdery and mixed with a bit of straw. yes use it once it is composted fully, or dig it waaay down in the soil otherwise it will burn the plants. another alternate is to apply it as a tea. let it sit in a 5gal bucket for several days, then dilute and apply. I would just start by getting a really good composting heap of it going, and also looking for the oldest stuff on the horseleavings situation. If you can get a bit of hay etc mixed in mores the better. Your soil can use a really healthy dressing of compost every year and in my experience horse manure can bring really really big worms that work the soil like 26 ft deep and such.
