Good adds to the list, Randy! BTW. Ulysses by James Joyce is not the same as Homer's Ulysses (which is probably the one you read in school). Joyce's Ulysses is considered by some to be the greatest work of fiction written in the 20th century, while others think its an overhyped pile of drivel. Its generally not taught in High Schools in America because for the first 40 years or so of its existence it was a banned book (for being too explicit about sex). Most people who read it have to have a second book at hand (a Key) to figure it out, because its written as stream of consciousness from the point of view of an Irish Jew walking around Dublin on June 16, 1904, and there are tons of deep references about Joyce's life, and the life of Dublin as well as mythological, biblical, biological and historical references layered in there. See article about Bloomsday in Wikipedia. Yes, I did teach English at one point in my life...
