Speechless. Wow. It's another theme or cable running through it all. The dawn of the internet. The home brew computer club. The rise of Silicon Valley and its immediate spill over from the Blood Alley highway to Santa Cruz for that daily commute. The mind-set that spawned a "computer for the rest of us." Do you remember all those kinda awkward uber-geeks in costume at Northern Faire, visiting as customers, working ale-stands, carrying halyards, being part of the swirl where possible, soaking up the wildly diverse sensory over-load of a 1,000 years of the magical undercurrents of the human condition, then stumbling back to Hewlett Packard, Apple, where-ever, and hunkering down and designing those whoo-dingus things that utterly transformed the world in ten years? Don't try to tell me that the rise of information technology in the Bay Area of the 1980s was not in some curious respects directly influenced by the cultural diaspora spawned by the Renaissance Faires.

5 Greg "Grego" Dana February 02, 2009 at 05:00PM
Born of artpolitic literate idealism, somewhere along the way RPF opted for theater, hedonism, and popularity. Granted, the hedonism was an enlightened one, reveling in the hand-made, the human, sensuality with a heart. But the 20th century contemporary renaissance mulch RPF sprang from was just about to give the world a scientific kick not dissimilar to Gutenberg's most palpable boot print. No, not electric guitars or psychedelics. They were already happening, stripping us down to skulls and roses, naked, baptized for a new age. That age was destined to involve a knowledge/connectivity explosion. "Computer" still mostly meant IBM, but Richard was already involved through UCSC in some kind of tweaky satellite computer communication thingy, the internet was just a few years from busting out, and everybody could smell the age of aquarius lurking in the wings. Me and him and Paul (with Brother Bob Farmer we had birthed Danse Macabre at the Shire), would be hobnobbing about how the hullabaloo of the arts in the renaissance went hand in hand with spiritual and scientific awakenings. But that was little more than a footnote at the Faire, you had to read between the lines to find it. We were hobnobbing about clean renewable energy. It was a no brainer to tap into the historical hall of mirrors that reflects the repetitive patterns of our collective journey. Richard kept chasing the dream at UC, cruising the information superhighway back when it was a secret little dirt road. I was a company man at LHC, but decided to team with Paul to bring solar to RPF. He stayed in Santa Cruz, and, being a handy man with both calculator and coping saw, actually designed and built the mirror. Paul's mirror, (cut and paste that into your hard drives), was, alas, in the hands of artists. It never managed to find economic sustainability. Though it amazed and inspired many a hungry pilgrim during it's all to short lifetime, it died of neglect at much too young an age. There are some things beer, boobs, and huzzah are better at, in the short run. Thankfully, some of us still have our eyes on the horizon too.
