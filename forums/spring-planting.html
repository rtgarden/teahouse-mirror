10 [12qb7e04dtezw] March 21, 2008 at 09:15PM
I love plants. Today, I am getting the trellis netting up for my bodacious hops. When I first planted them, they were small and pathetic. The next year 15 shoots came up. Eight years out, and I can't even count the shoots. They look like little cannabis plants when they come up. The leaves especially. The best thing about them is that they grow so fast that they create a shady bower to enjoy by the time the summer comes on strong. Then they die back in the winter. I give the ripe baskets to my friends who brew. (they are organic Cascade hops, after all). We also use them to make a very bitter tea that, frankly, packs quite a buzz. (if you can take the bitterness) I remember drinking the ale from the Hopland brewery. It was extra potent, and Bob taught me that it was the hops. BTW- that's why Ranier Ale was the choice among that crowd. It was the hoppiest, and therefore the trippiest. (I guess we were pioneers in Trip Hop!) In a few weeks, the aphids will come on strong in the hop forest, but I will just release ladybugs. They love to live in the hops. In fact, a whole eco system springs up in the well- watered shade. The first winds of autumn make them rustle. They turn brown and go to powder. When I finally tear them down, I am always careful not to disturb the mantis eggs that always appear on the pergola beneath the broad leaves. We have a very healthy population of mantids. Attached are photos of one of my ferrets, enjoying the autumn hops. They crinkle and rustle quite a bit when they get dry, which is very exciting to a ferret.
