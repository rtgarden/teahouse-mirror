9 Eric Lethe November 26, 2007 at 04:19PM
We originally thought we'd have too many people at the first one (although it didn't work out that way). But I did some early work looking into pass production. The idea was to develop a guest list (which we also did on the Wiki) and to invite people in waves, requesting RSVP until we had nearly the right number (which is 150, btw...that's how many people you're allowed to have at that meadow in Tilden). Then we thought we'd mail out passes and require them for entry. Pretty simple (and only requires one person to sit the gate and handle will-call and pass checking). I should say that this is the exact method used at some camp-out parties I attend these days. The passes bear most resemblance to backstage passes from rock concerts (they are worn on a lanyard, laminated and very pretty...make for good keepsakes if you vary the design year to year).

Okay, this is really quick, considering I need to be getting ready to head out to Dickens, but in talking to people who learned about CHBP after the fact and who had the carrot of "it may be an annual event" dangled before them, the interest is high. CHBP flew under the radar for a great deal of the old Faire community (which was what I gathered was the original intention) but if we want to open it up more and get the word out, I think there will be greater turn-out and participation. It's a double edged sword, though. How to get enough people to make the effort worthwhile but not too many to make it unmanageable?
