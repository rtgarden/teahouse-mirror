I've generally seen yerba santa in the high desert. I don't think it is a northern California plant. Comfrey is lovely, isn't it? It can be quite invasive, though, and should be contained in a comfrey basket or something that keeps the roots from spreading too much. I've had a comfrey plant in a pot for years that goes dormant in the winter, but grows back every spring stronger than ever. Supposedly, comfrey leaves are considered poisonous, and should not be eaten, but they make a great tea (though I usually use the roots more than the leaf for tea.) In any case, comfrey should not be ingested for long periods of time, as it may be cancer-causing (this is up for debate -- hugely concentrated amounts of anything are not really good for you and could be shown to induce cancer in mice in amounts that no human could use in three lifetimes!) The leaves can be used as a poultice, and are considered by some sources to have antiseptic qualities as well as other general healing properties -- it is also known as "boneknit."

4 [2ao3o6xb6daou] April 02, 2008 at 12:34AM
Willow bark is also a good headache remedy, particularly the bark of white willow. It contains salicin, which is the active ingredient in Ibuprofen. I'm not sure you want to grow a willow tree, though! One of the best herbal remedies for headaches is yerba santa. Pluck one leaf, lick the back of it, and stick it to your forehead. When it falls off on it's own, it should take the headache with it. (I actually did this a few times in Devore, when hiking through the back washes, and recommended it to others -- once you get past the silliness of having a leaf stuck to your forehead, it actually does work quite well.) It's also good for putting a few leaves in with your water bottle when hiking in the desert -- it helps your body absorb the water better.
