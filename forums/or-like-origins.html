12 [xj4gqw5wi0c2] January 30, 2009 at 12:37AM
Just looked at a wikipedia entry to refresh my memory: Nov. 27 1978. Think my recollection fits. Crew setup at Cow Palace for Dicken's Faire. We were sort of playing around in one of the back parking lots having jousting matches with the man lifts that had just arrived from the rental contractor (known as "training"), and hadn't been pressed into service inside yet. A feeling of just total shock, denial, deflation. It settled over the crew morale regardless of feelings about the sexual orientation thing, and more largely about my God the mayor's shot; and I think the interior would have been coming together with vendor stalls and actor workshops about a week later if that, and just permeated. A feeling that it would affect the customers, would affect the gate, less money flowing around. Don't recall if that happened, but I do recall some kind of bleakness in the tear down and the company coming short of cash. That might have been the last Cow Palace and then it moved to the water front, or were we out at the waterfront by then?

14 Eric Lethe January 29, 2009 at 11:16PM
I think I told you the story about Chris Neely's Dad, which I heard from Chris while we were shopping for wedding bands at his house (now gone, alas). He had albums of clippings from his Dad's artist collective that built that house (adobe fashioned from local mud and straw) along with a lot of ceramics which they sold on State Street. I believe they helped start the Yes Store. They certainly had a killer 12th Night party every year, which Phyllis and Ron somehow heard about and recruited them (and their costumes) to provide atmosphere at the very first Faire in Laurel Canyon (which he contended at the time made him the *first* Faire Brat, since KPat was a babe in arms and Chris (and Reggie Hibbard) were actual scrambling kids by then). A search of old Santa Barbara newspapers should tell a lot about this group of artists and their early doings.
