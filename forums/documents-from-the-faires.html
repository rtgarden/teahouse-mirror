15 [31fbpnust8bpu] February 01, 2009 at 07:32AM
You can has cheeseburger if you just re-size or scan to around 120dpi for the web. Most monitors max out at 72-96dpi, so any more detail is wasted (if you or the publishing robot constrains the display in the or tags) or you get monstro displays that blow the formatting to smithereens. 150 dpi will give you decent results if you want to print from the scan or allow others to do so. Most printers max out at around 300dpi, so scanning to a higher detail that that is, for (re)printing purposes, wasted time and HD space. Put another way, an image that is 800x600 pixels will fit comfortably on most modern monitors, with some room left over for framing, sidebars, etc. Anything bigger will get shrunk by the browser/html display. Hope this helps as a general guideline. Here's a re-sized version of your post, taken down to 72dpi (800x1035 pixels). I think you'll see little difference in the display here on the web. The file size however is down from 13MB to around 600k.

20 PatriciaBlanco February 01, 2009 at 01:24AM
Okay, here's my first salvo into the fray: a full color scan of an advertising flyer for RPFS'74. To give this artifact some background, this (and others like it) were printed by the thousands and circulated in as many major shopping centers as possible before the opening of any given Faire. I first started out as a costumed "leaflet - er" around 1973 (when Ray Tatar was Entertainment Coordinator), and later, for S'74, I got the actual (hourly) job of coordinating the teams of costumed "leaflet - ers" throughout the greater LA area. Our job was to go to an assigned area (Mall or shopping district) and, in costume, merrily convince the shop owners that they were *very* lucky indeed to have the opportunity to post a flyer in their front window. With a BIG smile, winkie-eyes, and other entertaining subversive techniques, a successful "Leaflet-er" could cover a huge area, say like Rodeo Drive in Beverly Hills, or Wishire Blvd, in WLA. Shopping Malls were a bit more tricky, as we had to cousin our way past the Mall management, first, and *then* cover as many windows as possible. Paid quite well, actually, and was a whole lot of fun.
