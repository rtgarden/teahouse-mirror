The Westley Hotel, a few miles east of I-5 on Hwy. 33, has great food. Hwy. 33 was the main road through the valley long before I-5 came along, and there are many old towns along the way, some with real restaurants, shops, etc. From time to time when I've had the time, I've cruised 33 instead of 5 to see what's there. The Westley Hotel is the best I've found. It's at 8615 Highway 33 Westley, CA 95387 (209) 894-3663 Also, the Cattlemen's steakhouse in Livermore is great, but only 1 hour out of S.F. Hakim knows a great taqueria off of Hwy. 99 around Fresno, I think. But it always seems to be closed when I go by there. :-)

9 Greg "Grego" Dana February 17, 2008 at 05:24AM
I don't usually give shameless plugs for commercial establishments ... especially those where the air all around smells like cow sh**. But Harris Ranch, aka (in our family, at least) "thousand cows," is really just the best stop on the 5 between the L.A. area and the Bay area, bar none. The bathrooms (in the main facility, not so much the gas station) are always clean and spacious (big consideration, at least for me), the coffee is actually fairly good and for years it was the only place to get a non-Dennys style (or worse) cup of coffee before you reached the Bay Area, and the food in the family dining restaurant is good, if not cheap. Even though I've been a vegetarian for more than 25 years, I've always found something good to eat when we stop at 1000 cows. Now, driving away from, or past, Harris Ranch, that a different story. Roll up your windows and put the AC on recirc. In the years before I had a vehicle with AC, I always tried to make the drive at night, when the smell was somewhat less obnoxious. Buttonwillow? a good place for gas (see my "Kids with Gas" post)
