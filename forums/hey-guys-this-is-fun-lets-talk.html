I already posted on How I Came to Faire on the How You Came to Faire thread (strangely enough). So I thought that since this thread was about Catt's pals from all over, I'd post about how I met Catt. I know, of course, it was at Faire. But I couldn't remember working Faire and NOT knowing Catt, other than probably my first Southern when I worked at Children's Dell. I can't honestly recall whether I met Catt through Jane and the coffeehouse family, or through the entertainment family and workshops and all that good stuff ... or when I was hanging around Comedia 'cuz I thought Kimmieann was hot (okay, she was hot) or when it was. Anyway, being one of Catt's pals and moving in intersecting circles at the Faire, that's just how it was and I'm very glad is was. And I'm so glad you started this wonderful teahouse where we can all reconnect. I seem to recall that you traveled and were away from Faire for a while, then you came back (w/ Apple?) ... then at some point, I got rooted in So. Calif. and lost touch we many of the folks who took a pass on the transition from Agoura to Berdoo, and then when DB stopped doing the coffeehouse in Berdoo, there was nothing more for me to do at Faire for a long time and so we just visited Faires once or twice a year, and so lost touch w/ more folks. Then Lucia invited us on to Teahouse and wow - lots of old friends and faire family reconnecting. Thank you!

Hi, I'm Raven, I was born Ravenmajel Liane Williams, but changed that to Colleen Liane Williams when I was 7, due to a tradition of name changing in Subud, a spiritual brotherhood my mother raised me around. But then at 17 I realized I was never going to be normal, so I changed my name back to Ravenmajel. When I got married 11 years ago, I took his name Usi. But when I get divorced (filing with the next paycheck), I will take part of my grandmother's maiden name which was Margaret Playfaire Andersen, wanna guess what part I will be taking? I don't want to go back to using Williams, because I was molested by my father at very early age, so I want to put that out of my mind and misery. But the pain my parents caused me is oddly balanced out by the spiritual tools they gave me in creating my life such as it is. My association with the faire for one, it was my parents who first took my sister to the faire, and she eventually brought me out. let me start back at the beginning, a lot of you have heard this or parts of this story before. According to my father (ROBERT J. WILLIAMS, gone now since '94) he was a technician for KPFK in Los Angeles back in the early '60s. He helped to maintain their Mt. Wilson outpost as an electrician and ham radio operator. He was also a mixer for various musicians like Paul Beaver. He participated in the faire as long as it was a benefit for KPFK, and he claimed he knew Ron and Phyllis. He and mom had a booth selling grape juice with dry ice in it and calling it love potion (that is according to my mom and Hadassah). Mom was pregnant with me at the '65 faire in LA. Then fast forward about 8 years. I'm now seven and Hadassah Foster, my sister is 16, dancing with a troupe on Round Rouge stage in Agoura. That is my first memories of faire. I was just a little kid running around by myself, I vaguely remember children's dell, but mostly I remember sitting on the stump at main stage watching a belly dancer with a snake wrapped around her middle. Fast forward again to when I am 16 and it's 1982. My sister is now head cashier, and she thinks it's cute to get her kid sister onto garbage crew. It was either that or parking lot. But I wanted to be inside the faire. so I ended up as a Privy queen that year at southern. At northern mom and I managed to get a gig living on the lot, but we weren't actually working at faire at all. We just stayed there during the week guarding a booth, going into berkeley for allergy treatments and fucking off on the weekends. That lasted for three weeks till I got really sick with strep throat and mom had to drive me all the way home to Humboldt (we left LA when I was 9). Then I didn't do faire again till I was 19, worked at the salad booth for at least four faires, then for Ray Griswold at the hats adorned booth for a few faires. By then I felt we were starting to beat a dead horse and I took a few faires off. Promised myself I wouldn't come back till I could do what I really wanted, massage! Worked for several years in the early '90's' at faire,till '97 I think. But once they left Blackpoint, I didn't have the heart to keep doing it anymore.
5 Murray then, now aka MAX November 22, 2007 at 11:46PM
Well my first faires were some of the first Faires for sure. I was pretty small and mostly we visited. There was a particularly heartbreaking session with a broken cookie and a spilled glass of milk. ONly Indus Arthur could soothe that scraped knee. At eight I signed on as the youngest folk dancer in a new troupe started by my godparents kids. AMAN! was an instant hit and suddenly I was dancing in huge halls and out at the Faire. This lated for a few years. IN 1976 I went out to Fire top get my own job, Lori Rose hired me as a washerwoman. The nextyear I helped to create the Mongers guild, and eventually I became a stage manager. Meanwhile i worked after hours also at the teahouse serving late at night and early in the morning. Eventually I had Appolonia and I went back to some street work. Just after that I split for University returning a couple years after Bob died on the brink of the last Blackpoint. For about ten years solid we have been flogging the dog pretty good getting a tribal dance scene going, creating little hideouts and special outlandish events and times. Hopefully these new tools will make it even easier to get together and have some fun!
