Indeed now that I have "grown up" and have something to contribute to the conversation, I am getting to know folks I looked up to when I first began faire. And if I may offer an expansion on your theory. The first part of it is actually belongs to Silas: When we did 3 shows a year we did not need the internet to keep up with those in our community. I call it the "accordion of friendship". We are all connected and sometimes we are close, but other times the music of life pulls us in different directions. We will always grow close again, so should never fear being pulled apart. For decades I only knew Veronica (RPFS Court) in passing, such as, "please pass the champagne". Then she put the word out for a traveling companion to Europe summer 2005 for a dance festival... and now I cannot imagine life without her. Many people here I am catching up with again because I moved home after years on the other coast for grad school and career and now I actually bump into them in pubs and Berkeley Bowl.

For me the beauty and joy of these teahouses is in meeting new friends who know my old friends from another side. It seems I missed out on a lot of good friends in my travels. One of Dundii's favorite stories about Gertrude Stein was when she met a person who knew many of the people she knew, but from different times. She knew the beginning of many stories that he knew the middle or end to, and he knew the other parts of many stories that she knew. Over the course of time, they filled in a LOT of blanks for each other. We are doing that here, too. Also, I am getting to hear stories and get to know about many people and things that happened after I left Faireshire. For all of which, new friends, I am most grateful. I'm making some green tea. 10 Murray then, now aka MAX December 05, 2007 at 12:58PM
Interesting Raven, and true. Usually the only time that we have had to get to know each other is when we are working together on a project. Of course now we are ALL working together on a project...or rather just hanging out and cagging whatever way you see it. The other exciting thing for me, is that instead of just talking about the photos and videos and songs and memories and writings and everything that we have been creatively working on all these years...we share them and enjoy them together now. Its a neat whole next step. When we gather we do not spend all our time chatting about how to get a hold of someone...instead we have real time to be together as if we actually lived just down the street again. Like a teahouse. When I first started using the internet it was in this context. Tom and Adam Long and Hughes and I all got internet accounts so that we could keep talking about our mutual interest in taoism. One of the first things that I noticed is that when Adam started touring for reals (like 11 1/2 months a year) I still felt like he was just down the street from me. Heck even now it is as if he is still right down the street...even though he has been rooted in London for nearly twenty years. Raising two kids. With any luck when we all see each other at parties and gatherings we will actually remember each others names now. Not a bad side- benefit. How many times have you said "I know your soul but your name eludes me?". We also get to know that *soul* other side of each other...the more interior landscape. Inherent in the process of these tools is the slow revelation of character and culture that I so adore. There are a couple of people in particular who I knew back in the day, however our contact was so surface oriented from doing the show together and always being under time pressure together that I am just now starting to get to know them really as people. Really as the sort of heart and sight and word and bond that we are made of. Its exciting and its one of the funnest art undertakings I have ever taken on. Doing my best favorite thing with my best favorite people. In the past I have jumped through flaming hoops just to have crumbs of time with many of you people....even the ones I am just meeting now...all worthwhile. This teahouse gives us the opportunity to share ourselves in a whole new way! we are all making it up as we go!
