5 Murray then, now aka MAX May 11, 2010 at 08:18PM
Here's a belated reply to several of the above postings -- Harvey Levine and Michael Levine were not related. And it was Herbie Goldberg, not Harvey, who did falafel. Harvey did the bread, cheese and sausage booth most of his faire years (though he started in '71 as a wanderer selling "Pleasure Squares" -- odd tasting wrapped little carob candy squares), and took over the b,c, & s booth when Mildred's son Richard gave it up after a brief try. And yes, it was Michelle and Suzie Storch who did quiche. Later they went more public with it, selling to supermarkets and large chains as "Michelle's Quiche." And yes again, it was John and Lois Solomon who did the Dickens onion soup (still the best I've ever tasted). All of these folks were VERY dear people, as of course were most faire folk. Thanks for reminding me of them. I miss them all.
