3 PatriciaBlanco October 21, 2010 at 12:23AM
Hi Murray: The Sacramento Dickens Christmas Faire (aka 'freezer faire') was in 1991, and held at the California State Fair Grounds. The event actually went through most of the weekends scheduled, but just before the last weekend (literally on the Friday night before) an Alaskan cold front came down the coast and, yes, pipes burst all over the Northern California area. I was in Davis, CA (15 miles +/- outside of Sacramento) at the time, and while the DCF did actually *open* that Saturday morning, it had to be shut down about 90 minutes into the day, as the fire sprinkler pipes were totally broken, and the Fire Marshall declared the situation too dangerous to have any public events there until the pipes were fixed. Needless to say, the pipes did not get fixed until spring of 1992, long after the DCF was long-long gone.

I think you're spot on re: Dickens at the Fox. If I recall correctly, '84 was the year I was trying to establish myself in Marin (didn't work -- had to move back to SoCal a few months later). If you took the Bart from the City, you got out just in front of the old Broadway dept. store (I think) just across the street. The Broadway always had several big guys on its side of the street working security, and we had Kenji (sp?) and whoever he was partnered with. Kenji and / or other Security guys would often get escort detail taking folks back to their cars, but I got drafted for that a few times (I was not actually working for Security then - I was working Front Gate, which I believe was sort of its own dept.) ... but never alone. What I don't recall is whether or not there was a Dickens at the Pier the following year or there was a gap.

So, here it is, based on the SF Chronicle: Locations for Dickens Fair 1970 Unspecified wharehouses near the Embarcadero near Union & Battery (first faire - 3 weeks) (I think it was the Old Icehouse, now gone). 5:00 to 11:00 pm, Tuesday through Friday, noon to 11 pm Saturday & Sunday. The article in the S.F. Chronicle mentions Jay Paul and James Kahlo, as well as Consortium Antiquum. It also says that Ron & Phyllis "have done a total of 12 Faires, here and in Los Angeles" Yikes! Such babies. My favorite quote from the Pattersons references both fairs, Renn & Dickens: "Our favorite periods and both times when there were queens." Well, yes, and....LOL. 1971 Wharfside, next to the Cannery, Fisherman's Wharf. As I recall, this was when the Cannery was still in construction (more or less) and the fair was in the base of warren of brick buildings. Cold as hell. I was 13 or so and went with my sister in costume. Thursday and Friday, 5-11, Saturday noon to 11, Sunday noon to 8:00 1972 Army Street, next to the produce market. Weekends, 11-11 Saturday, 11-9 Sunday. 1973 same venue, Saturdays 11-9, Sundays 11 to 7. 1974 same venue, Saturdays 11-10, Sundays 11 to 7. Think I went to this one as a volunteer. 1975 same venue, Saturdays 10-10, Sunday 10-7. Volunteered at this one and worked Fezziwigs ad hoc. 1976 no fair that year. Some promo stuff for the Emporium or Macys, and a ball at a college in the East Bay (Mills?--Aurie might remember), but no fair. 1977 The Cow Palace. Friday and Saturday, 10-10, Sunday 10-7. I worked this one dancing and those were loooooooong weekends. I weighed 135 pounds at 6'2" from dancing so much and got hammer toes. They really worked your butt off in those days. I've never worked so hard in my life! 1978 The Cow Palace, again. Didn't work this one. This was during one of the first major LHC/Faire meltdowns and there was a lot of bad blood at that time. I was sulking. No more Fridays. Saturday 10-10, Sunday 10-7. 1979 Fort Mason. Saturday 10-10, Sunday 10-7.
