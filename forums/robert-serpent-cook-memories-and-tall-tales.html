4 [20yvgj9ib8e2q] August 17, 2009 at 02:18AM
I think I put these in a letter to Robin years ago. It was the night before opening of RPFS in the mid-to-late 70's and there was the Serpent bent over a sewing machine in Bob and Margie Wright's living room in Chatsworth. On one side of the sewing machine was a stack of cut out groom suits, and on the other side a stash of Coke and candy bars. I fell asleep to the sound of the sewing machine's whirring. By dawn, the Coke cans and candy wrappers (now empty) had switched sides with all but one of the now complete groom suits. Robert finished the final groom suit as we were packing the cars to go. He then worked a long, hot opening weekend on little or no sleep and never showed a hint of the exhaustion he must have felt. When I finally graduated college, having crammed four years into six, the Serpent honored me by attending my graduation party. There was a large wooden hot tub in the back yard and Robert was the first man in, and the last man out. I don't know how many hours (really...hours) he floated like a toothpick in a water glass in that tub, but grinned the whole time. People came and went around him and he floated on, undisturbed. I miss him. There are more stories...maybe another time. D

10 [3mwymznoikrr9] August 09, 2009 at 10:47PM
One morning at Northern Faire (the only year I overlapped with Serpent), not long after the gate opened, I came across Serpent (I assume as Ralph Sadler) presiding over Pied Piper Court (I think that was the name of it), settling some dispute between two peasants. Surprisingly he stood there in his Elizabethan underwear, and as he presided he was slowly dressed by his servants -- the audience getting an intimate history lesson as they watched the whole process of a nobleman getting layered in and synched up. This was not in any way a private moment played for atmosphere, Robert played both the court and the dressing full out to the audience, pulling them in and engaging them, both a spectacular and subtle piece of theater that went a long way towards "making it real."

Serpent had this huge fake-fur cape that he wore after hours.A gaggle of us were Howling at the Moon one Sat.night up on Procession Hill( SRPF ) , including Serpent and his cloak. We watched, amazed, as he & the cape purposefully morphed into a condor...and THEN...he went just down the hill, where a small group of unsuspecting young persons were camping. He ( in his condor person) hunkered down and then asked them ( all in a most friendly fashion ) if they were good to eat. Being Faire Folk, they replied that they were stringy and gamey and not good to eat at all. Cathy, I am so happy to re- connect with you. I miss Serpent, and think of him often.
