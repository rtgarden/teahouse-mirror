2 Greg "Grego" Dana September 22, 2010 at 06:40AM
Life is good. In 1982 I discovered I could live without white powered stuff and booze. In 1983 or '84, I discovered I could even work Faire without white powered stuff or booze or any other chemical assistance. Since 1982, I haven't knowingly ingested a mind altering substance that was not administered by a doctor or nurse, or on very rare occasion, prescribed by a doctor and taken as directed. And I have worked Faire many times without chemical assistance; previous to 1982, I didn't believe I could do that. I don't know that I have anything big to brag about, but I have been married (to the same person) for 21 years, my daughter is 19 and in community college (both wife and daughter are here on teahouse), we somehow manage to pay the mortgage every month, and I go to work every day at a job where I really like my job and the folks I work with. May not be the most exciting job in the world, but the far from the most boring, it pays the bills, and they realize the importance of family and make accommodations accordingly. The first time I came in to tell my then boss that I needed to take my daughter (then 12) to the doctor he said "So what are you doing standing here? Go take care of your family; this will be here when you get back." Gail and I are once again doing Southern Faire, building and managing Yanna's booth for her. Some folks at Faire think I am a saint for working for Yanna (for trade) for 3 years. More probably think I'm a sucker and they're prolly closer to right. Rowan has been working S. Faire since she was 12 or so; when we were not going, she went w/ trusted family friends. The next year she formally joined Clan MacColin and stayed out at Faire every weekend (w/ guardians that we've known since long before she was born). We have a house in Van Noise, CA that was built in 1947 and I have a list of "To Do's" on it a mile long. We have two cars at least 10 years old, each of which from time to time develops a hole into which we need to pour $$. My old Crew shirt fits Rowan. I look at and wonder how it ever fit me. I will prolly never wear the size pants I wore in 1982 or 1984 or even 1989 when I got married. I have a mountain bike in the garage ... same one I had at Faire for a while after I sobered up. Every time I try to ride it now, though, my knees protest mightily. Life is good.
