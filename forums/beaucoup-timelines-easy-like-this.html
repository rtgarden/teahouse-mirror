8 [1m2dkokp1oiys] February 22, 2009 at 07:16AM
When I worked Northern in 1981 I didn't yet have my license, let alone a car. I lived in Daly City and would get up at about 5:00 am in order to take Sam Trans into SF, transfer to a MUNI line to the SF Depot, in a very nasty part of town, and then take Golden Gate Transit up to Novato. From there, like you, Richard, I would walk along the railroad tracks and pray for a ride. I learned quickly that if I wore at least my faire shirt with my jeans I would usually be spotted by another worker and given a ride. I never had to walk all the way out to site at least. I never did this alone; I was always with my sister and other friends. When deciding whether or not to accept a ride I would often ask to see a gate pass to confirm they were a participant (future security guard at heart even back then!) One particular guy looked highly questionable, and when my sister and I asked if he worked the faire he said "What fair?", to which we said "Bye!" A moment later a lovely young Scotsman pulled up who recognized me and offered us a ride. I recall one time walking along the tracks and hearing a very loud voice saying to get away from the tracks. My first wild, completely absurd thought was that God was speaking. Of course, I quickly realized it was a CHP officer trying to make sure my friends and I didn't get flattened by a train. Riding Golden Gate Transit was always interesting. Sonia and Lani and I would sit in the back singing bawdy faire songs and war protest songs and writing poetry to pass the time. On one occasion some young girls kept staring at us. Granted, if the singing didn't draw enough attention, the faire shirts certainly did. Just as the kids were getting off the bus, they looked at us and one of them asked "Why are you running away?' After we stopped laughing, we realized that our clothes were a bit dirty from spending the day in workshops, and we had backpacks with us. In retrospect I realize just how risky a way it was to get to faire, especially for teenage girls. We were lucky indeed.

But we might get some milage locally out of idiot-friendly keywords, So here's an update of suggestions for both, abbreviated tags followed by keywords (RPF and DCF probably stand as is, eh?). MAJOR EVENTS RPFSXX (eg RPFS77 for Renaissance Pleasure Faire 1977) RPFNXX (Renaissance Pleasure Faire Northern year) DCFXX (Dickens Christmas Faire year) OSFP17XX (Old San Francisco Pier 17 year) OSFWFXX (Old San Francisco Waterfront year) SDXX (ShinDig year) DSXX (Drake Show year) OCXX (Old California year) PRTXX: party (a catch-all for famous after-hours recreational events; spam feed, bubba bud, too much fun to remember) What other "main show" designations would we need? CREWS and DEPARTMENTS could be shoehorned into CRW: crew STF: staff or more detail: SS: Service and Supply (maintenance) BEV: Beverage (and Plumbers) CARP: Carpenters PLC: Parking Lot Cags MEC: Mechanics ENT: Entertainment SEC: Security FIN: Finance PS: Planning Staff (what did they do in those offices all day long anyway? Is Finance part of that? Should we call it "BR" for the Brass, or BS for Bosses?) DEC: Burlap & Decor PROPS: Props (Maybe deco falls more in this catagory, maybe DES for "design") COS: Costumes BTH: Booth (If more specific, CAT: Caterers, GA: Games, CR: Craft, ALE) CUS: Customers IOU: People who never payed me back What departments have we missed? FAIRE COMPANIES INDY (The early days) TEL (Theme Events Ltd) LHC (Living History Center) REC (Renaissance Entertainment Corporation) SHOWS STR: street show STG: stage show (If more specific, MUS: music, ACT: actors DNC: dance, GLD: Guild stuff) NS: night show CS: crew show TIME FRAMES will come in handy. When we don't know exactly: EXXS Early "decade" (70s, 80s) MXXS Mid... LXXS Late... PRE: Pre-Faire TD: Teardown OFF: Off Season, between shows MISCELLANEOUS DOC: Document (the search engine will tell us file types, but scanned documents will show up as photos) LIST: Lists (duh)(vehicle names, lingo, births, ...) ART: art (crafts, signs, sets, t-shirts, buttons, ...) RIP: ... 23 Murray then, now aka MAX February 08, 2009 at 03:53AM
It wasnt really that many miles from my high school to the show. Agoura high was even the staging for hiring I think once or twice. As a child it was the strangest thing to live next to, I grew up one more offramp down the road up Lindero Canyon. The faire was close enough away that eventually Simone and I would even ride our bikes up there. The faire was there and not there. It was like it was swallowed by earth. I grew up like all children do outside of seasonal festivals understanding the cyclical aspects of seasonal time and temporary space. I first worked faire as a child as a guest participant with AMAN!. I already understood that it was faerie land. I was always the akward kid at home on my street, yet somehow I led this double life for a couple of years and I performed as a dancer with AMAN! And I became a musician at school. Theatre and its continual mystery of transformation were revealed to me in spades. At least tastes. I wanted more so I got my first job. Bless Laurie Rose you should have seen the look on her face when I approached her on the very last day or workshop weekend with my girlfriend in tow. She just sort of relented. That year went by in a strange dream where your mom drops you off at the top of the driveway and you sort of march into this bubble of cheer. BY the next year Mama Lee had taken me in some and I had a sort of staging at her majesties home for wayward women (and girls). By then I was in High School Proper and KImi's Poppy paul would brave the guards and drive us all the way down to the bone yard where he would turn around his blue vw van and deposit us with proper parental flair. It was a big wide world of Faire. The cast was phenominally huge and already there was tis thick sense of an interior fairytale. A sort of rockstars and players among everyone that were unknown to the outside world. Like people that were too busy having fun and making stuff to care if the rest of the world followed their whimsy enough to be celebrated. These were artists and performers celebrated among and by their peers, and that is what they craved, and that is what they yearned for, and that is what was delivered faithfully served in big steaming bowls of dust with sun motes and cheering crowds and banners waving all above. The faire was an intensely psychedellic thread of other self and other world dancing with several in a sort of braid around my year. After southern we all went to the strip on the weekends for midnight shows of Rocky Horror, a continuation of my own sort of "adult" life at faire. Because I had learned my attitude out at the mainstage backstage and with people the caliber of Terry Collier I never cared too much what other people thought of me on this very basic very needy level that turns people away so most of the people on the strip were sort of instantly curious about me. The riot house (or HYatt house) was right down the street and the end of the beginning of the rock trip was still pouring out into the other parties. I never went or hung out it was not my thing. I would play cards with Michael at Wizards T shirt shop or see how fast I could get kicked out of Ben Franklins. So a drive by commute was the sort of surreal pusuit that being a kid in Agoura I had sort of cut my teeth on that. Nothing prepared me for the rabbit hole down the crazy place experience of the Actors Bus to Northern. Once Glad and I had managed to plot our way into monger reality soup we also plotted to get me and Kimi (so her dad would drive us to Glendale) onto the bus. Immediately Terry made me his bus aide, which meant I was supposed to make sure someone was awake when we hit all the freeway crap in the east bay after the Kettleman city stop so we didnt end up in like Sacramento. This was an actual real and serious danger and something which YTerry had great experience in. Its one thing to arrive on the lot at 1 or 2ish and trundle off to bed with a show to do the next day, and quite another when you arrive at dawn. So I was terrified. This kept me pretty sober and gave me an excuse to avoid most of the potential trouble. I am pretty sure that was the very moment that we started calling Marc "Animal" because he was doing this spot on version of the Muppet character one seat over. This was not like stepping back in time, this was not like being transported to another dimension, this was like being hit right in the face with a large wave of ludic spray and being tumbled ass over teakettle onto the beach. Gasping for air, gasping for a sense of self among the sudden appearance of so many people oddly *like* me. People who laughed at my jokes. By the next weekend i was already absolutely flashing alive at every nerve and so aware that I can still taste the pavement outside th liquor store as we exited the Glendale freeway in Glendale where the bus was getting loaded up at that stage. The air was already pulsing with this strange gold and then silver light and I could hear the blood pumping in my veina. A warm rush of familiarity and sensation and acceptance and wonderment and familial tang hit me all at once. It was not just a commute, it was a magic spaceship ride. Right this moment I can remember almost every single minute of it all at once. walking down the street to buy soda with Kimianne and seeing Terry Collier exit the freeway in his little white bug, cap in place and intent look on his face. Walking the long block back to the parking lot I could easily recognize Glad Pickering's swagger even though at a distance it just looked like a fifteen year old boy standing confidently in a Glendale parking lot. Out of the wood work the magic players would come. It was like a game to see how fast you could spot people as they made their way from the straight world to our magic spaceship ride. Every weekend was different. Every day itself was epic. Jokes were born and ruled and thrown down by new jokes sometimes five in a day. Someone would forget a towel or a shirt and so they would wear something funny on their head on the way back to their tent from the showers. I learned that if you rocked it right it always looked good. I learned how to not fall awake or asleep on the bus so that we never once got lost through the tangle of mislabeled and horrendous freeway ,madness that stressed us every single time. The commute was a magic rocket ship ride, and it revealed early on something to me that I have always tried to keep in mind, Our worlds collide. So one part of the week I would be walking the institutional halls of Agoura High, sneaking cigs behind the Drama building or more likely ditching school outright and holding court at Denny's having coffee. The other part of the week I was in an other world, a place where the horns were in the air and the ground smelled like chamomile and soft hay when you walk on it first thing in the day. It was back in time and it was forwards in time and every now and again I could swear we went sideways real good and the minutes stretched to hours and the hours stretched to days and the days were like months and each season was like shedding a skin and becoming a whole new person and then pulling the whole of coastal California through the eye of a needle with us and dancing on the head of a camel just to prove that they also had been remade anew It was like we got turned inside out so that everyone else could get turned right side in. This is a hard concept for most people to grasp, and one i carried with me in a special sort of secret place. After I attended my first dances downriver in Hupa, dances that have changed little in form or location in maybe 30 or more thousand years, that i started to grasp that we were actually having some sort of giant ant colony like human colony like behavior. It was intense because it seemed like even the places we were gathering had been used prior to us to gather...moreso Agoura. Ronnie Wilson once told me about growing up in West Hollywood with hit surfing records on the charts and their own worl in full tilt boogie. He always felt like he could relate to me because when he was a kid in school they were cutting class to surf with hot chicks and hearing themselves on the radio. They had plenty of cash so they even rented their own apartments out at the beach just so they would have a place to party and shag and shower and change clothes after surfing before they would head to school at Fairfax High. Partially it was unique to our experience and partially it was something that is part of old ways that we have of moving on the land. Horns have been raised in the air here for a long long time. the first time they danced out the white deer skins (and grey ones and a few brown) at a Jump dance I really cried because even though most of the guys were wearing Boxers under their deerskin flaps, and there were logging trucks rumbling by just a couple hundred feet from the dancers, the right feeling was coming through. sometimes you travel outside of time.

the point is not to sanitize the experience , however to put it in perspective by framing it in context. Many many of us left the show because of the drugs, the altered states and the decadence that was therein. In some ways our story mirrors the story of the introduction of LSD to popular culture because Bob and Bear personally engineered it, engineered it within our midst. Intentionally. We were manipulated. We were kids too. It was cool...we were easy...I get it. Did you know freeway rickie was supplying us with columbian? yes the same guy who supplied inner city black neighborhoods with crack for conintelpro. We were put up and set up sas more ways than one. I know for sure about that one and it has always made me wonder about the possible loss of my best friend Sally (an important artist in our midst) to a serial killer who used a cyanide capsule rather than be captured. There is more to our story I would love your help in sorting out. There was even another disappearance around the same time. Personally I dont want the story to just read "drugs make good art". I dont also want the story to read ":drugs ruin everything and control your life". Later Bob had his own regrets about opening Pandoras box how and when they did. He told me that it was important to remember that they had given people a premature view of what was possible. He felt that they had used training wheels too long or revealed a door where there was not one yet. He felt it was more important for people to pursue the inner work themselves to reach some of those states. Spirit was the order of the day. Many people in very altered, often self-medicated states have caused havoc as well as wonder to ensue. The story sits over on wikipedia already totally sanitized. We are not responsible for that. The phyllis patterson story, which many also assume to be our origin story (despite the contribution of thousands of other people to those seminal agoura years) is a story that has been entirely crafted without regard for this spirit of inquiry and self exploration that I see as the heart of the drug experimentation that many of us were undertaking. Its not my place to *out* anyone for their drug use. Really I have no idea how the statute of limitations really works these days. When I went to jail as a political prisoner they changed the laws so that once I came out everyones rights were so reduced it felt like i never got mine back even though we plead our way out of it all pretty slick (too sick for a trial). Personally I dont want to publish a book and then end up in jail. I also feel that we need to look at our situation from an educated, broad anthropological and sociological view as well as a historical one. There is more than just history at stake. We are shaping the vision of a next generation of youth (mostly who are unseen to us) who really admire us from afar. That is a tremendous responsibility. If you learn about this stuff in the underground, you see it very much like Sas says. Take a social situation with lots of young people, put them in a semi wilderness situation, add lots of new brain chemistry and inhibition removing stimulation, and you get a festival. Some people say TAZ is the way i disagree. not. There were also ideas at hand. Consciousness didnt come in a straw. You can actually move much faster and jump much higher without the distraction and manipuation of exogenous drug sources especially metabolically addictive ones. The entire chemical factory resides within and can be reached quite intentionally. There are other simpler ways. Personally I am interested in revealing the ideas underneath it all, while giving a nod to the influences of an era. We weren't the only one guys... ever been to a Hollywood movie from the time? For awhile (afterwards) the best place to get work was the Sunday meetings for AA and NA at the Hollywood Presbyterian. And here we are. Living in the aftermath. About to tell all in a tell all? Phyllis is a good example. Historically she is this odd bird. A woman at the head of one of the biggest dog and pony shows of its era. An ice breaker and a maverick. Would anyone expect her to be normal?
