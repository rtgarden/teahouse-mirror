20 Eric Lethe April 05, 2009 at 05:49PM
I already asked Ning...yes, all Rosemary did was delete her own account. They believe it is the right thing to do to allow folks that kind of control over their data and in most cases I agree with them. What makes Teahouse a potential "special case" in my mind is that people are sharing pictures and other assets that they "own" in the sense that they have them in their hands, but they are historical assets from our point of view. Another data point is that our new relationship with Ning means that we could have had 100% of Rosemary's content archived on our own server...which means in future we will have the option of retaining everybody's content for the greater glory of historical aggregation. If we decide to do this it will be a deviation from the terms of service our current 700 folks have agreed to (so we (Teatopia) will need to go through a one-time explanation and buy-in exercise with our existing community....and need to consider developing a future "terms of use" for this community that makes it explicit that any pictures you post will be archived privately). IMHO, this is the only responsible way to handle archiving, which I think we all want to do, right?
