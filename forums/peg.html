Peg took us all to her heart and managed to remember each and every one of us by name. I always got chills (and a tear) when she would do her line during Queen's show about how England may have had better princes, but none who would love it better. Whew! My Peg story is a two-pronged one. I was one of the Queen's chair bearers when Mark Lewis was the captain. We hoisted the sedan chair on our shoulders for most of the route, but would carry the chair low in our hands with our arms straight down on the road where trees overhung the route. One time, Mark decided that when we came out from under the trees, instead of carrying her low until the next Progress stop, we would hoist the chair up to our shoulders while in steady motion, like a hydraulic lift. On his signal we did it, with her doing the Queen's wave. The look on her face was priceless as she felt the chair go up and tried to recover her composure. The second prong of the story is this: At the end of progress every Faire day, we would bring her chair to the ground and kneel in attendance until she disappeared behind the burlap. Each day, she would improvise a little speech just for the benefit of the few of us who were there for the very end of Progress. It was theatrical and corney and very sweet, and we all loved her for being every inch the Queen until the flap of burlap hid Queen Elizabeth who would then become Peg Spinwell for the remainder of the day. God bless our dear Peg Long.

Excellent advice to read the interview with Peg (and as a bookend the 1979 B&M). It explained a lot to me (why I left Faire at about that time--if they could do that to Peg, it could happen to anyone, so WTF was the point) when I read it. In my humble opinion it was more or less downhill from there insofar as one type of Faire is concerned (and not to belittle the great work done by Luisa, et al., in the subsequent years). It just wasn't "my" Faire after 1979. Okay, enough about me. I've told my Peg story before, but it bears repeating for the sheer Pegosity of the whole thing. RPFN, 1976, the Queen's Show. 3:00 p.m., Novato. This very event is memorialized in Luisa's posted video which is marked 1977 but is, in fact, 1976. My friend Kathryn Fletcher (all of 17 or so at the time) was shanghaied into filing Peg's cup during Queen's Show (Hillary Ayer was ill, I believe). We all knew that Peg liked a little something "extra" in her lemonade (!), so Kathryn, dutiful girl that she was (and knowing how to mix a mean cocktail at her tender age), filled the cup with about a finger of lemonade and the rest vodka. Because that was the way it was done, right? Well, needless to say, Peg, the complete professional, did the cup proud and downed it. But the look she shot to the assembled Court as she turned away from the audience would have peeled the paint off of less hardy souls. The show went on, as did the progress, but needless to say it was a rather intriguing Glade stop which ensued. To Peg's infinite credit, I don't think Kathryn even got scolded. It was an honest mistake, and Peg, bless her heart, just went with it. Be sure you watch Luisa's video. I was shocked when I saw it because Kathryn only perfomed this function once, ever, and it was captured on film. Great times!!!!!

8 Greg "Grego" Dana February 23, 2009 at 04:34AM
I have so many memories of Peg. She was, to me and many, the heart of the Faire for many years. When I started as backstage guard, I believe Peg was still Queen and Director. I was the guard at back of Main Stage @ Agoura and back of Props at Black Point. As many of you know, we had a bit of a problem with yellow jackets, especially at Northern. One of my duties was setting and fill "yellow jacket traps" -- one gallon apple juice jars w/ 1" lemonade syrup in the bottom. Another part of my job was keeping actors away from the traps, but I couldn't be everywhere at once. One year there was a guy who was "liberating" the yellow jackets by tripping the traps so the wasps could get out. There had been several very serious incidents with allergic people getting stung that year, so of course, I was a little upset, especially when the guy was caught. I of course wanted to run the guy out of the Faire, and would not have minded at bit if he "tripped" several times on the way out. I was ready to call walking guards to escort the guy off the lot and there were practically flames coming out of me ears. Peg came out of the container that was serving as the Entertainment office and somehow calmed the situation down with a few words, came up with an consequence that was appropriate without losing an actor, and made me okay with the whole thing. What a remarkable woman.
