9 [i0g57c50dbqj] March 16, 2010 at 01:56PM
Back in June/July of '02 I had the dubious pleasure of being the guy who did the drawings for and did part of the installation of that gate and the telephone entry box...when the General Contractor found out I "knew stuff" about the site I became his New Best Friend for the day. They really had no clue as to what anything was -- didn't even understand why the landscaping they put in and redid and redid kept shifting about as the water levels changed under the old parking lot peat. He allowed as how they were especially confused when they discovered the actor's showers. He also confessed to having had a great deal of trouble removing the water pipes and the water truck J-stand in particular. During the times I was there, measuring, taking pictures, and generally proceeding with the gate installation, I watched them carve away at the base of Cardiac Hill and place these huge boulders to hold the rest of the embankment in place to create the turn-around outside the gates. A luscious blonde vixen drove by on a Cushman, towing a canopied full bar trailer....very good G&Ts which made what I was doing feel less painful. The only building I recognized then was the Scale House...but they had moved it all the way out to where Larry V's trailer had been. It appeared they were using it for the Golf Course Maintenance lot office. The folks who have moved in to the houses for whom I feel bad, are the ones who live above and behind the Ben Jonson, and those at the End of the World. In my X-number of years at that site I found these areas to be the most, shall we say -- 'apparitionally active' -- ? I know from experience that the Natives get particularly restless on occasion, and it isn't always pleasant....but that's another few stories.

16 Eric Lethe January 27, 2010 at 06:35PM
Thanks for the report, Paula. Did you take any pictures? I've been out there myself several times when you could still drive in without trouble because they were still selling the houses. That "attractive cement pathway" is for golf carts, and the standing water you saw is probably why you weren't harrassed...not many golfers play in the rain. I agree that its worth going if only for the smell in the unchanged areas, especially in the Fall. The swampy nature of the front-line area was always there...we just worked to suppress it in order to use that land. The people who built the golf course were required by Cal-EPA to return the marshes to their natural state (and to keep the Caravansary area untouched). Sadly they traded that for widening Witches Wood, which took out several oak trees. Ah well... I'll be interested to hear if you have dreams about it now that you've seen it recently.
