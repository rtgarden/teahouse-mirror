58 Greg "Grego" Dana December 24, 2008 at 05:54AM
Two of the greatest moments in the history of world theater came from "Pie-a-Boss". In Agoura Bob Brewer was too much of a man to take it in the face, and all agreed when he turned and revealed that such a moon is rarely seen. That the pie hit the mark so perfectly was the best proof I've ever seen of Gpd's existance. Bob spent the rest of the night suffering for his art. Then there was the time when Mitch's number came up at Dickens. There was a pin-drop silence for the little eternity it took him to realize he held the winning ticket. But when his "heh heh heh" announced the miracle from the back of the house as he made his way forward there was no mistaking the source of the sound, or the significance of the moment. The reaction from the audience welled up slowly like an outside wave as he closed in on the stage, but by the time he hit, the walls of the hall bowed from the tsunami like force of the hellacious roar. Was that the Evil Dwarf he was aiming at?
