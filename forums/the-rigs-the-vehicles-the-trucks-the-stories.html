Ah Big Red, I remember the first year in Devore, Big Red was shipped down on a flatbed and someone needed to unload it. I was the only one around in camp. The truck driver had backed it up to a retaining wall down in the river bed and expected me to back it across the 12" gap with 4 of the 10 tires hanging off the edges of the flatbed. Now I had never actually driven it before, I just knew how to start it. So of course I was an expert. "Hey you should know how just in case there's a fire and you're the only one around" said the head of mechanics in Black Point a few months earlier. What was his name? I stomped on the starter peddle just the right way (a little to the right with some clockwise english on it), It started, I jumped the gap and grinned ear to ear all the way back to mechanics.

27 [2ao3o6xb6daou] March 03, 2009 at 05:58PM
Orange Crush, the garbage truck... The red security pick-up truck with the emergency brake release that would paralyze your hand for the rest of the day if you didn't get immediately out of the way... There was another white pick-up truck with manual transmission that they gave us for awhile down in Devore -- I was the only one on deco who could drive it, but the seat wouldn't move up, so I'd have to climb under the steering wheel to push in the clutch while Karan steered from the passenger seat, laughing her head off, because it looked like no one was driving... Then there was the "Fido" series -- the baby blue volkswagon bugs that Therl drove around the lot. One Monday brunch day at Black Point, when Therl had tippled a bit too much by mid-afternoon, he drove off. A few minutes later, there was a loud "whirr, BASH!!" Everyone from the coffee house went running, knowing that Therl had just crashed. We found Fido on it's side, with Therl looking like a little leprechaun, sitting cross-legged on the side door that was now the top of Fido. He had gotten to where the high road and the low road split, and couldn't decide which road to take, so he took them both! He wasn't hurt, Fido wasn't hurt, and a bunch of guys tipped Fido back on it's wheels, and Therl drove off happily...
