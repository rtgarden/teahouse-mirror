29 PatriciaBlanco March 14, 2009 at 02:49PM
Okay, I see the Marich name has come out of the bag. Here's the saga. In approx 1974 Steve Marich showed up at an Anglo Folke rehersal we were having in Claremont. Richard Chase was still directing us and Phyllis had sent him over to learn dance. I believe the Kahoutec comet festival was also happening at the time. Steve only attended a few, less than 5, rehersals. Some time later Steve called me at the D street house in Ontario. Steve asked about learning Morris. I gave him our schedule. No,no. He didn't want to come and join with us. He wanted me to come to him and teach some guys he had. See, Richard Chase taught me Sword and Morris and I danced with the guys all the time, Uncle Dick just wouldn't let me perform in public.Heck, first time Doug Burger learned a sword dance I was 1st sword.Back to Steve. I was still confused as to why he wanted me. Steve said Phyllis wanted a Morris team for faire. Well, great, I told him, she has one. We had been working on morris for a few years and if she wanted a team we could put some dances together for her. No,no. Phyllis didn't want our guys. Well, why not? Steve claimed that Phyllis said our guys weren't good enough.SLAM! Wait Steve, you're telling me you want me to come teach your guys Morris because our guys weren't good enough? YES! he exclaimed! Uh, I'm sorry Steve but YOU tell my guys that. You won't come? he asked. No Steve. Then I hung up and called the guys, I think it was Doug Burger I called first.What a difficult time. That Spring Steve had his ballet teacher at workshops reading out of a book teaching a bunch of fellas morris. They were all paid, they were all given costumes produced by the costume department, and Marich's Merry Morristers was born. You never saw so many pointed toes in your life. Richard Chase was furious. I believe it may have been his last faire. He returned the pewter goblet Phyllis & Ron had presented him. Years later I found that goblet on a dusty shelf at the farmhouse at northern. After Uncle Dick left we changed our name to Caca Fuego.We hurt so bad.But we struggled on. Peg introduced us to a fiddler named Dave Ricker who joined us with another fiddler named Rita Bradis. Steve's morris team disappeared. And the guys dubbed our morris team Pipe and Bowl.Then Steve Marich reappeared. This time having copped the gig of Richard Jenkins. Jenkins was as fine a Cornish man as walked the earth. He was a friend of Richard Chase, and his gig included the Hobby horse. Imagine my horror to see history repeat itself this way. I met with the troupe back at actor's camp, which was under the big top. I told them I was going to punch out Steve and that I would probably be 86"d from faire. Then I headed into faire. I found him and called his name. Steve turned and, seeing me, his face broke into a smirk of malice, gloating and distain.For some reason my fist changed to a open hand, I guess I felt it more appropriate. My hand started somewhere behind my right hip and wound up somewhere to the right of Steve's head.A huge slap. Which today I will say was wrong of me. Steve howled, and swore, and started calling for Phyllis and security. He swore I'd answer for this. I said fine, this all has to come out, and the sooner the better. Following him we wound up at the security base which was located with the pass house. He went in and a few minutes later Bob Kamen came out. "You the one who slapped Steve?" Yes Bob. "You know I've got to kick you off the lot?" Yes Bob. Then I could swear his eyes twinkled. "You'll be showing up for work tonight?" Yes Bob ( I was weekend night security at actor's camp). I was directed to meet with Phyllis, Peg, and Steve on Monday. I showed. It was rescheduled to the next day.I showed, It was rescheduled again.I showed it was rescheduled again. Then Peg met with me, told me to never do that again and informed me that Steve had left to pursue his dancing career in New York. We didn't see Steve again for 10 years. When he reappeared in the late '80's a couple of people approached me and asked if I'd smack Steve again because it seemed to have worked before.No.that book was closed and they'd have to write their own chapter. Whew! Glad that's out and over.

I had originally joined the cast because I was supposed to be a fire dancer from Crete! The original costumes for that had body stockings under the bare breasted Minoan dress style.
