5 [2dm7p3rumw1co] July 15, 2009 at 06:36AM
I remember Joe quite well, he was one of a kind. The bar was always dark and smelled like old beer and cigars. He had steward sandwiches that he would heat in one of those old pre-microwave ovens. He always had a cigar in his mouth so he talked kinda like an old gangster. If you drank there then you got used to a few cigar ashes in your drink. He was old school, if you were of color he would serve you but, he broke the glass after you left. It's funny, he really seemed to like the crew, he treated us fairly and allowed us to have many partys there. In fact Ron C. thew me a party just before I got married, what fun. The houses were something else, holes in the walls, missing doors, I don't remember if there was a stove but, I do remember a five gallon propane tank outside with a line running indoors. Bessie was a good person Moat was a trip. He would either be sitting behind the counter or in his living room. There was an opening in the wall behind the counter (window size) that would look into their living room. He was not much of a talker But, Bessie made up for that. Many of us would have gone hungry if it wasn't for those tabs, she was a very nice person. I remember more than once she asked me to help her collect on some overdue tabs but, I think almost everyone paid her as quickly as they could. I'm not sure what she did with all the comps she got but, I think she hit everyone from the faire at least once. When they sold the store the new owners made many improvements but, they didn't make it better. Here's to you Bessie, Moats, and Joe you're not forgotten and still remembered fondly

I always remember the time McGowan got thrown in jail for thumbing a cop at 2:30am on Atherton Road. He'd been visiting DeBorba's and decided he was too drunk to drive home, so he decided to leave his car in Novato and walk back to the Lot. But along the way he was thumbing cars that passed (why walk when you can ride?). Unfortunately one was the local cop, who proceeded to ask for his ID. Bob had some tickets he was late in paying, so was prepared for random ID checks with a fake ID. As I remember it, the guy whose ID fake he had turned out to also had some type of record (or maybe Bob was just too drunk and admitted who he was? Maybe Val can help out with this one?) Long story short, he ended up spending the night in jail instead of safe and warm in his trailer (where he would no doubt have been if he hadn't been so responsible in the first place not to drink and drive). Sometimes proximity to Novato was a pain.
