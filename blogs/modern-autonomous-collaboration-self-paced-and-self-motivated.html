Modern Autonomous collaboration...self paced and self motivated
By Murray then, now aka MAX | March 08, 2008 | 0 comments
This is a piece I wrote for my livejournal which is mostly internationals and scientists as the audience. Since I learned nearly everything at the start about collaboration from many of you (or from the same dearly beloved departed teachers) I thought I would share it here in the teahouse as well. sifted into place. Now that several key things are fixed, here, salvaged...whatever...things are getting into a new pattern. The g5 stands out now as the "entertainment center" since it interfaces with itunes and has the DVD player. It definitely surfs a little faster than anything else, its all limited by the connection and the speed of the internet itself of course so there you go. It is shocking to me at times how nice it is to use the OLPC XO and again I admit to using it almost every day even with the g5 fixed and the Ubuntu at 7.1 it is simply an easier object to wield. I am working out a 30 second spot to give an idea of the OLPC project through animation. No publically available 3D models have been made yet (that i can find), so my first chore was transposing the color chart into materials in blender.Asset management is key with animation, and this is a good project for me as it helps me work out the keystroking of it all. Next was the Logo. Essentially the main idea is to create a 3D blend file to share with world, with all the basic assets needed to get started, the "user" icon logo, the logo from the computer itself (which is not outlined the same way), and some sort of version of the rig itself. Of course notes on the project style guide and color uses also need to be made within the blend file. My hope is that once I have the basics including some uv mapping done, someone else will take it the next step and really make the little lappy up in 3D. Essentially this is my way of being able to contribute to the project right now. Hopefully some other assets will be created by other people and by the times my chops are up to it I can use them in creating more short animations about the project. Its a way of repeating the tutorials with a direct goal in mind. Using blender is funny, it is like playing the piano to achieve a direct object goal...like if you play really well this certain piece you get a bowl of soup, or an umbrella, or something equally deliberate and precise. Sometimes it is soup that you really do not want to look at again let alone eat... just the same the act of playing the instrument in a way that uses the intellect produces direct desired visible results. Important to mention that deliberate visualization and construction is the key. It would be very difficult to start out wanting a bowl of soup and end up with an umbrella on accident. You must know what you want to make at the start. Its odd because I can start to see my own hand in my own 3D work now. When I even made the very first olpc XO logo up it looked to me like one of my own paintings. I have always been very "super flat" in my work, and the colors are very similar to my own palette in a couple of past painting series in particular. AS a part of my eventual blender certificate I will probably work out a tutorial using this project as a main feature, these will be the distributed files for anyone taking my tutorials, and it is possible that I will make one soon on asset management and workflow issues in Blender 3D. It would also be great to work out a modeling tutorial since I know that in the start the octopus and penguin tutorials get a little old. Back to the Many Merlins project, perhaps it would be neat to make a crystal ball, then you could work out how to put various effects on it like disorted reflections or interior images or smoky plasma, or even the small animation of the OLPC XO lappy. As a teacher I am somewhat obsessed with the idea that the work should build in a consecutive manner which includes certain skills and exposure to abilities that the student may not understand that they need further down the road. This must done either with great stealth or total firmness there can be no weakness in this regard. Your students depend on you after all...if they could learn it without you they would. In general the secret to an art process is to walk yourself through it in a semi inspired or totally inspired way. Usually there is a sort of proto piece that sparks off this inquiry which we call "Series Work" and which traditionally in the past has resulted in an art "Series" which is usually shown as a whole in either a gallery or a museum to allow the public to interact with the ideas and the artists personal area of inquiry. Collaboration in this regard can take two basic forms. The first is the very literal direct translation of the collaboration into physical work. One summer during the CSU Summer Arts program at HSU I did a lot of collaborations with a team of other painters including my ex-husband Pat, my pal Jamie and Lily a young painter from the bay area. We would actually all paint together on one big canvass, including painting out others peoples marks and painting over another idea and all of that really daring do stuff. It was the essential psychological state of that sort of dependent collaboration. We were used to painting side by side so it was a sort of interesting shift. At that time there was no other idea of collaboration. It was so much shaped by the film process, and then again by the festival process, that it was very mutually dependent.Even the process of group long form improv (The Harold named after my departed pal Hal),rock bands, the process of large scale parties...you are all in one boat together even if in those forms you have more individual autonomy. The other form of collaboration which was slower to reveal itself was the more modern web 2.0 collaboration where there is no direct dependence on someone else's ability to meet a deadline or be inspired. Its like the difference between making groups do assignments together and simply having them work together. If they are expected to collaborate the dependent way, the a sort of dynamic emerges quickly based on who cares about getting the work done, and who has attention seeking activities that include passively refusing to participate. Instead of allowing everyones faults to limit the project the idea with the second type of collaboration is to allow everyones strengths to rise to the occasion and to have the reign of confidence lead the creative process. Working independently yet offered critical support and ear for each others work is part of an underlying principle of some of the work that has gone into the OLPC XO project, it is a perfect emblem for this idea of learning and creating at your own speed and comfort levels surrounded by helpful supportive people of all ages. This is the collaboration that the modern internet has fostered. Sustainability is a good example since there has been no single organization representing the idea that has managed to work together disseminating the concept. Rather it has been the combined efforts of an untold number of people carefully articulating and demonstrating this important goal...to create a mainstream conceptual shift that would be inclusive of what were formerly known as "alternative" technologies and "freegan" values. At this very moment in history we have an amazing opportunity to extend the power of constructivist education through the availability of meshed networked inexpensive laptops, the XO has everything cleverly in place to allow either form of collaboration (open source and university projects here being the key).. The development of things like the Tata auto which may extend a safer greener form of transportation to those living in 3rd and 2nd and 1st world conditions (after all they are all mixed up now because of global cities aren't they?) we may be looking at a next generation which may have higher education extended to them as never before seen in the history of known human kind. With free universities and free textbooks the principles of education could be endlessly extended. Without having to leave the villages opportunities may open up for women and children and men, at least the opportunity for the children to have school is a great motivator for the movement of many people displaced by war and its resulting famines and destruction. What would the dignity be like for a refugee camp to have along with shelter a small laptop as part of your personal gear? Mesh networked together...like sugar crm. In a situation with a orphaned population that is largely located in hospitals like the Rroma, or even more so like the Rroma not truly orphaned at all just separated from parents and family by the system of hospitals and the state these laptops could mean a healthier state of mind and greater autonomy to get social needs met appropriately. Perhaps there could be some small miracles of epidemic avoidance by realizing that most people should have control over their own health records, definitely they should be able to access them in a timely fashion which is often not possible. In bush hospitals in Africa for instance, the computers could allow doctors to keep track of patient medication use, or past blood work while also helping keep the patients calm, mentally active and connected to family. By extending the laptops we are extending the arena of collaboration. Because the style of collaboration is largely more independent than it was formerly we are not weakened by this extension.. At some point a larger meta playground will emerge. Currently this arena is extended in the form of a digital "playground" which is how the squeak environment is seen and felt by the kids once they are able to interconnect. The idea within etoys and the Squeak interface is that the "kiddie" part of the playground should have soft barriers that can be broken through to a larger playground. The etoys istelf is a powerful language, once you want to go further than games with it you simply learn how to activate the more advanced "playground" status where you can create at the advanced level. It is similar with the simcity game on the XO Don Hopkins re-designed it so that eventually you can use the assets and principles of the game to re-fashion it into your own micropolis game. There are even cheats for the game written into the notes in the source code to encourage people (children) to examine the code. At some point a larger meta playground starts to emerge, where the cross point of the sustainability and DIY and Open Source and OLPC XO ideologies meet. Largely they are all very related because they involve this sort of collaboration that is self paced and self motivated.
