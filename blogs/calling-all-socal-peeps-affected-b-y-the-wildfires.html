2 PatriciaBlanco September 23, 2009 at 03:21PM
Fire update, after 28 days the tragic Signal fire is expected to be fully contained by tomorrow. Now we have new fires, started yesterday. The fires in Norco, Redlands, Temecula, and the Riverside county "Riverbed" appear to be readily controllable at this point. But the Guiberson fire is more problematic. It started near Fillmore, in south east Ventura county, yesterday morning and quickly spread. 9.600 acres in less than 24 hours. 10% contained. It has moved from a mostly ranch area and is quickly headed toward the populated subdivisions of Moorpark. The focus today is structure protection. We have mild Santa Ana wind conditions."Red flag" warnings, meaning hot weather and winds, are posted. Whew! Gimme my El Nino now!

Monday update. News is not real good. 1) The Signal fire aka Flintridge/ La Canada fire, doubled in size last night up to 85,000 acres. Worse yet, we lost 2 firefighters last night----prayer pause----- whose truck went over the edge on a mountain road. Evacuation areas increased this hour to include parts of Sunland ,Tujunga, Acton & Palmdale. Two folks who did not heed mandatory evac. notice tried to ride it out in a hot tub. Mistake. On a report I heard from Battalion Chief Harris this is mostly a fuel driven fire, meaning it goes where the fuel is. Usually in this area there is 30% dead growth, but drought & smog has increased this to 50%. Also a factor, topagraphy, meaning fire burns uphill, and folks this is mountainous area. This is also a jagged fire, meaning it does not have a main leading edge. 2) The Morris fire, above Azusa, 2,100 acres burned,is 95% contained, Hooray! 3) Cottonwood fire,Hemet , 2,400 acres burned, 95% contained, Hooray! 4) Yucaipa fire, near Oak Glen, still bad.
