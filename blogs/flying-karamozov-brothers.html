12 [xj4gqw5wi0c2] March 09, 2009 at 04:36PM
I was there that night, Grego describes it perfectly, I remember the blue flash softly unfurl under the burlap like an aurora borealis, then thinking the whole thing was going to explode from the dust. How could you forget such a thing? Anyway ... So, there I was ... many years later, living and working in Washington DC, maybe 1992 or something, had grown fond of the Wolf Trap national center for the performing arts, most readers probably aware of it. During the winter, performances in a huge renovated barn (not unlike what Red Barn aspired to be). I took a friend to see the Karamazov Brothers perform, an intimate stage setting, kind of reminiscent of "you know what" and you just know where my mind was at. I couldn't resist. During the intermission the guys were hanging out around the stage chatting with audience members (this is the original group mind you) and I strolled up, complemented them on their fine performance and offered my opinion that if it hadn't been for that night at Ren Faire long ago, they probably would never have been kicked out of the next and gathered their collective wings which had taken them so far. They kind of stared at me, chuckled a bit nervously, looked around, said something like "Um, you were there, huh?" Says I, to the effect, aye and your secret is safe with me. What a great way to come full circle!

The current show was impressive. Paul is impressive...he's in his 50's now and still in great shape. The other, newer brothers look to be in the late 20's maybe early 30's. I got the scoop on the whereabouts of the now retired brothers. Randy works for Pixar. Sam retired to take care of his wife who was ill. Unfortunately she passed away and he stayed retired. Howard is retired and in school studying Biology and Tim retired as well. Paul will someday retired and become the director of the franchise, but I'll tell ya....watching the JAZZ bit was all the more impressive BECAUSE of Paul's showmanship, and experience. I do have to say that even though the juggling was excellent by all the new brothers, there is a certain bonding and panache that Howard, Paul, Sam, Randy and Tim had that just isn't present anymore. Still, I brought 18 friends who had never seen them before, and they thought it was MARVELOUS!
