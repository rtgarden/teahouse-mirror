fluxed up (ranty)
By Murray then, now aka MAX | April 23, 2008 | 0 comments
Everything is changing very quickly. even before i wrote that down it changed some more. My own goals are shifting somewhat. It looks like by the time I am in grad school or I can pull a fullbright the olpc may be something else. It will be very hard to stay with them if they keep losing key people. I am very hopeful that Jepson and Pixel Qi will have a touchscreen unit by then. That would be even better for the Rroma kids so they can use a keyboard that makes sense...the screens will allow infinite types of keyboards to be available so they can even switch between dialects and scripts. They can even make up their own. my internal goals are shifting. I am in contact with an old friend and that is part of it. We are both working on getting our websites freshened up. he is supposed to put music up for me to hear...and well...mine could use a dose of coherence or something. Something about 3D and open source and sustainability. So much of my time and mental energy has been behind the sustainability idea that now that it is raging I need to warm myself by a new fire. Open Source has been filling that need for awhile now. connecting the two ideas has been important. My life is sorta on the brink of something different. My oldest daughter is getting married, my youngest is getting ready for school in a year. It is hard for me to envision myself getting very far from the river for very long even though grad school is upcoming. I am starting to think about San Jose and its daunting but real to try and reach the very heart of the community thinking about multimedia as a method of idea sharing. Its the web page thing that has me spinning. I need to re-organize how I think about my work because I have changed. My work with the teahouse has really enriched my perspective on my working style and long term goals. I see now that I have been entrenched in a sort of participatory movement for some time. Folk dancing as a kid, in many ways I really learned from those late night fights over banquets of food and lots of good california wine while AMAN hammered out a methodology of encouraging folk dance until it really did take off. The Faire was like that also. Peg and Dennis had a secret world of plotting where they were using this construct of a faire and actors and a show to show us how to all participate in our own lives. How to show up. My first forays into science came because Chaos theory promised graphics would become feasible. I learned early about Unix from a pal.I got a vax account and i painted and waited and learned photography and i remember the day yahoo got graphics...I got on "The WEll". I paid the phone bill to prove it (660$ one month...the portal was in Sausalito...still area code 707 back then ma bell and all). We used it initially and always as a communication tool between good pals. The crazy part is that i have stumbled on a medium that is enjoying a sea change. It has a decent ecology and it is open source so i do not have to sell my soul to one vendor or platform or machine (it runs pretty well on low-end machines). Top-down (pixarish) studio style animation is not the only way to go. Remember tim burton's little toy animations on pee wees playhouse? Anything bootstrap is appealing to me. Paper dolls and hand sewn barbie clothes. It is as if suddenly you can make paintings on your own after needing a studio of five or ten asiistants. Previously say you needed a hundred oor a thousand in this case. Its mind boggling that we can all communicate this way suddenly. I am all about the idea of people sharing participation..I am a new games kid for reals. I come from an unbroken line of tinkers, including my dad the SA (codemonkey) and start-up pioneer, my grandpa the historian who helped machine the first jet in WWII. Before that it was the farm and the highway. Education has always proved our only way of really getting stable, even if it means doing something different than you thought. Re-education is fine. I was the first generation (in awhile) to consciously train for a job that didn't yet exist. Twenty years ago I envisioned that we would all have the ability to have in house studios. What we would do with them was another thing. Moving into 3D has been an interesting journey. It has been about a year and somehow I need to create a profile that reflects this tolerance of changing seas. I felt better after I did my yoga and darshan. Maybe I will walk shortly because I know that it will give me energy. Mostly I need to remember that I am enough, that as limited as my situation is I am doing enough.
