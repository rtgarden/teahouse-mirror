15 PatriciaBlanco February 19, 2009 at 11:04PM
Here is Kalia's story; When I try to follow the link to see the discussion, I just keep winding up here. Not quite sure why I can't get through. The goose in Question was named Goosolini, one of those giant, hissing, monster geese that give geese everywhere a bad name. On the day of the Attack, I was carrying a giant armload of bedding out to Edwin and Lisa's little trailer, which is where Jon and I were sleeping. In Gussolini's defense, the bedding was largely composed of goose-down products, but still... I had enough stuff in my arms that I couldn't see where I was going very well, and since it was bedding I didn't want to drop it in the dirt, so when Gussolini grabbed a corner of a bedspread and started whacking me with his wings and growling I was pretty much stuck. I seem to recall that it took a while for the other folks around to stop laughing and come get the goose off of me. This was his final attack, as it happened. They had had their eye on him for a while, and this one precipitated his being invited in for dinner. He was delicious. Later that weekend I made a decorated goose egg in his memory, showing Gussolini in full attack mode. I think Edwin and Lisa still have it.

20 Murray then, now aka MAX February 17, 2009 at 05:19AM
going out in the little boat, first thing in the morning just after Dawn with Liza (and of course after a strong cup of coffee freshly roasted on premises by Edwin for Liza), the mist was very thick on the hillside, river mist from the Russian river, and it made a tangible ground hugging soupy curtain that parted as we rowed through it: our quarry and chase so early in the day? nothing other than freshly picked Chanterells to go with our steak for breakfast. It was my first time ever finding wild mushrooms, identifying them and eating them promptly (something I am lucky enough to do every now and again( i know chantrells, hedgehog, oyster and maitsutake). We were looking for spots of orange in the oak leaf duff of the large trees across the lake from the summer house. Trees most likely planted by indigenous people who used that section of the golden trail. Ronnie Wilson told me that the lake was one of the few natural bodies of water of its kind in that whole section of California (non man made) and that the golden trail or Hyapom went right around it and along its merry way up the coast. My knowledge of the lake and of the trail is what cozied me in so quick with my Yurok adopted kin. We saw a little patch of orange, and Liza told me to carefully fan out "they are like a tribe" she said "Once you see a bit of one you know others are right around". She moved right up on the chantrell and right around it sure enough there was more. I was having a hard time seeing the other mushrooms under all the fallen oak leaves, and Liza said "look at the lay of the land and look for any bumps". Right away I got the peculiar sensation that I understood exactly what she meant and I could right away see three or four bumps clearly rising in the duff. Sure enough there they were! These were the wild kind that are bright bright orange. We picked about five pounds or so and headed back through the mist quite victorious, just in time to get properly chilled by the sun breaking through the fog. No matter breakie was due to be cooked on the kitchen woodstove which Eddy was quite good at working. Steak and eggs and chantrells, strong Ethiopian coffee roasted on the spot in a home-made roaster. 37 PatriciaBlanco February 15, 2009 at 12:12AM
I lived there for about 9 months in 1972,73, My son Rusty and I had the room above the dining room. It was a huge room, and a couple of the guys had helped built a loft for Rusty in the front corner. Rusty recently told me it was a real gift in his life to have the experences he had there. I remember one stormy night in the house when everyone was gone off to do Dickens, and it was just Rusty and I in the house, in fact I think we were the only ones on the hill. IT WAS SO SCARY! the wind would get under the tin roof and rattle, I was pretty new there then and didn't have a clue what I was hearing it was really spooky. That night I actually saw someone in the foyer, a man,in clothing of another era, and just as I was going to speak to him, poof, and he disapeared before my eyes. I ran back up the stairs so fast, got up in the loft with Rusty and laid awake all night listening to foot steps in the hall and on the steps. I saw another materialzation during the day a few weeks later. I have a lot more memories of Preston to share at another time.
