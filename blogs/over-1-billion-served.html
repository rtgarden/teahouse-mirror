over 1 billion served
By Murray then, now aka MAX | January 26, 2009 | 0 comments
As of December 2008 about a quarter of the worlds population is on the internet now. The advent of , and cell phone leapfrogging has meant that as of last september over 5 billion youtubes have been viewed. China specifically and Asia in general have the largest number of viewers. The 200$ laptop along with cloud computing has officially changed the game. Here in the US you can really feel the difference in sites like facebook which almost doubled in the last three months with users over 30 being the fastest growing demographic. Internet use is also revitalizing our libraries as community centers and in general getting people more connected, more involved. I watched the recent President Obama inauguration with a record breaking 22 million viewers using the cnnn facebook feed that let us watch each others status updates as we viewed the scene together live. The number of likely international web users is much higher than 1.5 billion since this study didn't take into account people under the age of 15. Reaching children with educational materials is one of my biggest projects on the internet, for me it spans over twenty years since I started collaborating with a group of other scientists on "The Well" to create a book that would have all books in it, all the music and imagery and video...that's how the laptop was created we wanted to make this object for the children. Originally it was called "The Dynabook". That was back when Bradshaw hadn't yet invented the fractal algorithm systems that let us view graphics on the internet. We used to wake up and check on yahoo which sites were new literally. There was a point where all of us scientists had read everything that existed on the internet, and the new offerings were the basis of the yahoo list. It is really the cell phone that is reaching everyone and tipping the balance. Dripping with social cachet the iphone offers a small touchable interactive screen to the internet a sign of things to come. Convergence is the reality...so many devices in one. You can even use the screen as a flashlight. The Android phones coming from Google are my special favorites. The operating system is Open Source and the phones themselves will come from a variety of providers in different models. For education in general Open Source is something we need to implement immediately. Vendor lock down has made many of our school computer labs seem pretty pointless which is ridiculous when the equipment is perfectly good (and easy) to install Ubuntu on. Open Source in general is a passion, we used to call it "GNU" or "free". Artistically I was raised at the knee of Bob Thomas who along with the Grateful Dead espoused an early model of content distribution and ludic spray in their willingness to have people record their concerts and use their graphic imagery in artworks. Bob's own image "Skullfuck" or "Steal your face" has been widely distributed and displayed without his own remuneration or involvement directly in the process. It was quite intentional he talked at length about it. Somehow sitting at the little spool table and watching the world flow in and out of our own proto social network out at faire he really grasped the potential of sharing. The cellphone can be a gateway to basic literacy as demonstrated by Berkeley's MILLIE program, it can also dramatically transform how our public spaces are decorated. Phyllis and I were talking at Danese's birthday about embedded murals. In Japan they have a system of QR codes that allow users to point their cell phone cameras at a piece of artwork and receive information about products and services immediately over the internet. This can easily change the pulp stream of visual culture in our public spaces. The possibilities for using public spaces as settings for gaming is also opening up with the potential to have activities, items and statistics all linked to places and embedded codes on the internet. The internet is expanding into the fabric of our daily lives and meshing together our involvement in bringing fast change to our daily lives in subtle and simple ways.
