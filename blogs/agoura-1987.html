4 Murray then, now aka MAX May 10, 2009 at 07:34PM
After I had been working for the Faire for a while, my mother told me I could never make a living working with my hands. I said "Watch Me!" She thought the Faire was all about wearing silly costumes and playing dress-up. Every year that I worked, I had the Costume Department adopt a "Mother", a person who was special to us or had helped us extraordinarily. Our "Mothers" included Deborah (Mac's wife), Faith Jansen, Ron Chordas, Lady Eleanor (Bruce Schwartz's puppet), and Wendy Newell.

10 Greig Fors February 24, 2009 at 08:10AM
Thanks Joyce, that brought tears to my eyes. I went through the same with my folks, they thought that I should make something of my life and be anything but an artist. They had the same feelings about faire and all the hippies and drugs. When they finally came to faire, they were also amazed. I think they managed to get to faire three times. The last time was in '87. You can see my mom in the stocks in one of my pics. I walked around with them as best I could because I had other obligations. But, everyone of my friends went out of their way to make them feel special. '87 was the last time they made it to faire as my mom had bone cancer and found the terrain too difficult. The kindness shown to my folks, I will never forget. I was fortunate to have her for another five years, she passed in '92. The one thing that I will never forget, is the first time they came to faire, I introduced them to Mark Wallis. My mom spoke to him for a few minutes, then told him that he had a great English accent and wondered how long it took him to perfect it. Poor Mark was mortified.
