22 Kathe Walters Scott August 23, 2010 at 12:47AM
Here's another very special TC memory - winter, the Black Point lot, cold and rainy and no one had any money left. It was getting very near to the bone, and then one night here came TC, banging at the door of my little cab-over, wearing a Santa hat. And when I had him come in, he disconnected my propane tank from the camp stove I cooked on, gave me a kiss on the cheek, and took off down Snob Hill, in a LOUDLY clanking van. A few hours later he was back, distributing "liberated" fuel all the way up snob hill to all and sundry. It made the difference between eating cold meals in the cold of that winter, and having hot meals and drinks, and a way to warm up the bricks I used to slip between the bedcovers to warm them up before sleeping.

27 Kathe Walters Scott August 22, 2010 at 03:04PM
One of the most important memories I have of TC is this one: It was the last Agoura Faire. Little Chris and I had just gotten together as a couple, and many of the Faire folks were openly critical, disapproving, and judgmental. A lot of folks who Chris thought of as friends basically shunned her, and we felt cast out of our family. (Some of you here might remember being culprits in this) I don't recall, now, which weekend it was, but it was between paychecks and Chris and I were really struggling. I had quit my "real life" job and decided to join the Faire full time, expecting to be held in the arms of the Faire community somehow. *rolls eyes* Chris had come down to the Pass House to talk to me and as were were talking TC wandered over from Radio Shack and cornered us. "When was the last time you two girls are something real?" he asked. And then without waiting for an answer, he said "I'll meet you at Radio shack when Pass House closes." And we did, and he heated up cans of soup for us and gave us brotherly advice and offered his home as a place to get away for a few days. (He was living off-lot at the time). And he never stopped being that kind to me. Ever.
