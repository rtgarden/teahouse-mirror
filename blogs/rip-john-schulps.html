Jon and I were booth neighbors for many years and friends longer. It was a joy to know him. I always felt more comfortable as a security guard when I knew Jon was working. The most memorable story I remember is when Jon and I were swapping stories and he told me about the time he had a run in with Charles Manson. He was working for LAPD (I think I have that right) and was called to a disturbance at one of the big parks in LA. There was Charles Manson. Manson at some point told Jon: "You should be afraid of me." Jon said that he laughed and told Manson: "No Charlie, you should be afraid of me." Jon chuckled as he told me that story. He had the best chuckle. I always felt like Jon was watching over us ... even after he no longer wore the Goon's shirt. When I was a young guard, working an area with no other guards by my side, I had confidence in situations that would have scared the crap out of me because I knew that with a few words on the radio, there would be a whole lot of us there ... or there might just be Jon and whoever his partner was. And that would be enough. And Jon's presence could calm down a situation like few people could. Jon would show up, and with few words, the trouble would walk away ... and everyone (including me) would walk away from the situation calmer than they were before he showed up. I never saw Jon get physical with any of our "guests" but I did see him make his presence known, in situations were others might have had to get physical.

Aah, John. You taught me so much. John was a man of quiet soul. Never too quick with a boast, it took a long while to get to know him, and there was always more to know.
