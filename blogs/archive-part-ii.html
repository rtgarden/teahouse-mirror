Fishy Oils and Flaxy Oils and Hempy Oils Sat, December 23, 2006 - 9:47 AM This morning, M requested "EYH(g)" for breakfast. So cool! My days online are now filled with more pages and tabs referencing apraxia and EFA's than anything else. I read up on omega plant sources this morning and found this from Dr. Robert Katz of the Omega-3 Research institute: "Flax seed oil or freshly ground flax seeds are an excellent source of the essential omega-3 alpha-linolenic acid (ALA or LNA) which is the quintessential parent member of the omega-3 family of essential fatty acids (EFAs). The body transforms it into EPA and the EPA into DHA. This transformation is very inefficient (the yield is about 10%) and is further inhibited by over consumption of omega-6 fatty acids from most vegetable oils or certain disease states. Therefore, it is advisable to independently consume also ready made EPA and DHA from good quality fish" I find this both interesting and disappointing at the same time as I'd much rather depend on plant sources but oh well. Hemp oil on the other hand (from Rawganique.com): Hempseed oil contains 54.4% linoleic acid (Omega-6), 18.3% linolenic acid (Omega-3), 2-5% gamma-linolenic acid (GLA) and 1-2% stearidonic acid; and as such is one of only 4 oils to do so: hemp oil is also the most economical of the group. It also resists spoilage much better than other plant sources. As for the log on M: He has been engaging in a lot more imaginary play. One game he likes in particular is "Action fingers" where our fingers "do the walking" like the old yellow pages ads and accomplish all sorts of tasks and play. We use our action fingers to clean up, practice commands like jump, run, walk, dance, etc. and they are our new favorite on-the-go toy. Gotta love action fingers. He has also been vocalizing alot more, playing his kazoo and whirly tube like a trumpet. I updated his vocab list this morning and when I looked it over, I was pretty impressed. Wed, December 27, 2006 - 10:48 AM - The demon hath emerged Sun, December 31, 2006 - 8:58 AM Previously unbeknownst to me, EFA's can have a negative behavioral side effect. This piece of information which I've only learned this morning has at least helped me to make sense of what we've been seeing the last few days. I now understand a bit better why my mellow little guy has turned into a fit thrower at the drop of a hat. This has worn Jed and I down quite a bit. A one hour trip to the grocery I took yesterday left Papa wanting to tear his hair out in the end. On the plus side, I am still seeing great things! Yesterday, he was playing with his back to me and started saying "DEEHG" at first softly and then louder until he was yelling it. He finally turned and ran over to me with his yellow plastic shovel, bent down to the ground and started "digging" while he excitedly shrieked "DEEHG" over and over again. It made me want to shriek excitedly too! I know all this may seem like nothing to a lot of parents but this is huge for M. He previously had made VERY few attempts in his 2.5 years of life to communicate anything with spoken language. Another great moment came yesterday afternoon when he and I were reading the gorgeous animal book that M's great grandma sent to him from OK. He climbed up into his table-seat with the book and beckoned to me to come and sit down with him. I took the book and we looked at while I asked him: "Can you show me the...?" We must have gone through 30 animals or more that he correctly pointed out before he got tired of the game. Then, we moved onto him pointing at the animals so that I would make the accompanying noises. He likes my turkey gobble a little too much unfortunately. His favorite noise to make is: "KEEK" for anything that resembles a mouse (including hamsters and chinchillas). Before, he wouldn't even play ball when we tried to sit down and read a book together. it had always turned into him grabbing the book and slamming it shut after looking at just one page. It was a refreshing change. I've also noticed significant improvements in his fine motor skills. The best example I can recall off the top of my head is walking into his room two days ago to find him putting his socks on. Another plus in this area is his recent interest in actually trying to use a spoon and/or fork during meal times. He's also become less opposed to wearing hats in the last few weeks. One interesting note in his language development, two babbles that I haven't heard for almost a year have returned: "eelahdeedyadee" along with "gilleeyagilleeya." The train table that he got has been a source of endless entertainment for him. The act of accurately placing the cars on the tracks or connecting the wooden track together is a bit of a challenge still but doesn't incite a fit on M's part (yet) so I am happy. Given his leaps in development, I have begun to scale back how much of the EFA's I'm giving him with the hope that his language development will continue with the same success without the ornery boy we've seen the last few days. As for me, I am trying desperately to find my copy of "Clear your clutter with Feng Shui." Apparently, it has gotten lost in the clutter on my desk but i am eager to find it as i need an accelerated kick in the butt to organize my life. I am very attached to re-visiting my artistic side that I've forced into seclusion and hibernatio0n. I can feel my spirit suffering because of this neglect but I feel I need to situate reevaluate my belongings and my surroundings before I can successfully approach this desire. My mom has a full sized floor loom that she wants to get rid of and it's making me dance inside. I never thought I would want to weave since I have always felt a strong connection to large scale metal sculpture. If i do get into weaving, I will want to make it a collaborative project using recycled textiles from anyone and everyone that will send me their "found" items and their project scraps. Both of these paths are now inspiring me to move on in my life.
