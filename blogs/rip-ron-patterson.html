In December of 1970(or was it '69?), as a 18-going-on-nineteen-year-old handsome young buckÂ I was brought to the very first Dickens Fair at the Ice house in San Francisco on a magical, drizzly, night and was immediately struck by the magical world I had seen. I immediately resolved to become a part of the event the next year, and, thanks to my best buddy Bob Furber hitching a ride from Berkeley to SF with Murray (Max) Epstein, found out that there were some jobs available making and/or hawking these ,little square confections called Pleasure Squares (from the brain of the late Harvey Levine...), we had our "in" - and thanks to "Passhouse Murray", as we came to know him, the rest is/was "Living History". I started working for Harvey making, and hawking & even, occasionally selling Pleasure Squares, the first time at Agoura that next Spring of '70. I made my mind up at that Faire that the best gigs at Faire were in the Ale stands, and thereafter focused all my efforts at job-hunting at the Beverage Department. As this was back before I was 21, so I couldn;t yet actually work IN an ale stand, (aw shoot!), and so I did the next, best thing, became a garbage man. I quickly zeroed in on the "most special" related gig, that of "privy prince" and I held that gig for several years, really enjoying the hawking at the customers to not be shy, go ahead and KNOCK! I think my favorite 'riff" went something like "'ere now, guv step right upÂ and knock! you might be amazed, but an EMPTY privy door never opens itself!" just to watch people's reactions ...priceless! So, I did that gig for several Faires, then worked Miscellaneous crew until I FINALLY made 21, after Northern in '73, worked my first ale stand at Ale 2 in Black Point in '74, under Ken Coberly, side-by-side with Don Mills shucking Perrier water from those ridiculos plastic "shrink-wrap 'skins on Â they used to pasckage 6-packs of Perrier bottles in, so they could be cooled and served individually. We'd usually start the day "shucking" a good 10 cases of those things, and then pouring beer like MAD for nealy the whole day. I rmember being set on a tap, and just pouring continuously through four or five KEGS without turning off the tap, except maybe to change a keg. Just pouring through a case of cups and screaming for more. Wonderful times! If we were lucky, there would be an occasional momentary "lift" to keep us going...(heh), and we'd have the Oak, Ash, and Thorn lads in to sing for us. Times I will never forget, I'd perhaps not be blesssed with my two sons and three major relationships of my life, were it not for the vision and imagination of Ron and Phyllis. Several here and over on Fscebook have said that it feels like the end of an era, and I feel that, too. But in a larger sense, I believe that we are also at a new beginning, we've had our own imaginations set free, by having experienced all that, we are now incited to grow that vision - furthur!
