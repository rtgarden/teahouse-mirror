Puppet Masters and Steampunk futures
By Murray then, now aka MAX | November 15, 2009 | 0 comments
(Billy Scudder as Charlie in the 80's: we have always been early adapters) Once upon a time there was an ensemble of people. They gathered and found each other like a mass of wild bees. Just as mysteriously as they arrived, humming and swirling and inspiring a curious mix of terror and delight they vanished. Only to regroup again many years later. Except it was more of a Portuguese Man of War than really ever a colony of bees. With siphonophore grace able to form a large complex organism, and with siphonophore grace also able to disassemble into individuals, and even reassemble into smaller "sailing" vessels that look like jellyfish and collect and recombine more like slime mold they made their way far and wide in the world. How does one of these masses form in the first place? They seem to emerge where possible and the whole process seems to be a game in certain ways. Playfully and with intricate and certain concentration a large scale alternate reality game or ARG as they are called today seems to be the converging point for this unique aspect of the human social colony. Paintings are made by painters, bronze statues by sculptors, images in silver on gelatin by photographers, movies by producers and ARG are made by Puppet masters. Its a curious pursuit, because the puppet master lives behind what we also now call the "iron curtain" which is the division between the gaming space, play and the real world creators and their intentions and methods.-They must have a secret life. The ARG usually has a sort of a message, like all art it has its own zeitgeist. Sometimes the zeigeist is "lets have another Renaissance", sometimes it is "play and learn while you laugh", or "investigate traditional technologies" other times our overall ARG method has shifted and chimerically become something else. A game can become a story and in turn can become a regular plain old show or festival or outdoor concert performance. (Leigh Brewer and Dundii in the early 70's) There is no college of puppetmastery. Probably you have to be a producer to be a puppet master. The first puppet masters of course did not even know that they were performing this important task specifically, they simply attempted to cobble together some sort of iron curtain so it was possible to function. They simply rode the serpent. (Phyllis Patterson in 1993, orginator and puppet master of Renaissance Pleasure Faire for 3 decades) The future looks more like the past than we thought it would. For years now some of the zeitgeist of our great games have been concerned with sustainability, with our abilities to be self reliant as well as connected and useful to the entire whole including all of the symbiotic connections. Our families became tribes layered and interwoven and inextricable. (Brian Patterson and Kevin Patterson: The Doctor and the Producer) Its not with nostalgia that we focus on the past, rather its the understanding that the future is an unknown that must be created. Our own role in the greater game is concerned with personalization, with creating our own character and ultimately creating who we really are. Our ability to move forwards rests less on our ability to create entirely novel technologies and more on our ability to mix up and mash up what we already have. To play through the scary parts. This is not limited to technologies it extends its influence as a metaphor into every corner of our lives. (Cat Taylor : Co creator of Dickens Fair, Producer, Artist Musician and Actress = Puppet master) So of course its our duty now to help to lead the masses into the great steampunk future where play and games are the methodology of a swift and rapid reorientation. There is no other way to turn the titanic of our modern consumer society or to avoid the sharp beak of the cracken of colonialism. For those of you carrying the great work forth, and to the puppet masters of yore who made it all possible: I salute you. Thank you. Carry on.
