the core is more limited in my perception than even the greater ensemble, someone could jooin the ensemble in a day just with the right attitude maybe join it in five minutes, hjowever to be part of the core you had to be more savvy. Also after a certain point the context shifted and being part of the core was sort of a selling point rather than a real thing. The core of the show became a new group of people at some point, partly the old crowd and that group to my thinking was more resposible for holding down the transition while the Faire became a national Phenomenon. In my thinking those are two totally different patters and chapters; the creation of the ensemble and fabrication of the original show is not something people can participate in per se any longer, the first big years of Arizona and Texas, and Burningman, created a new core which had a different culture. Peg felt that she had successfully disseminated her basic view of educational immersion, everymany history, Improv as social art, and basic bon homme to about 3,500 people (I think Richard was at the same lecture she gave about it), Realistically I think she managed to reach many many more than that just in those few years I worked with her at least 9,000 or so. Some days I think the whole crowd of 25,000 out at Agoura "got it". It wasnt just Peg who made the ensemble of course, however her hand was intentional, and she was very embracing and that had a deep effect on all of us and all of this during her tenure.

The number is kind of important to get a rough consensus to, because of the ways social networks and complex systems operate, at a certain point those notions of self organizing systems, innovation from chaos, six degrees of freedom start to kick in and help us understand from the distance of decades looking back, what on earth was going on. Yes Peg counted the crew and much of the boothies as active parts of the ensemble, she would always say 1400-1800 on a good day. Of those people she could be counted on to remember about 600 names, she taught me to remember the rest. She always claimed that she and Terry Collier were too old to remember names that that was my job. I would get that familiar elbow in the rib on the approach that meant I was to feed her the name (mostly she didnt care but sometimes she wanted to know for effect). In this case I would either tell her or take the blow myself..."what was your name again?' (as she acted distracted). Peg was full of tricks, that was one of my favorite things about her, and being a kid at the time it really impressed me.
