i deleted your earlier comment Rosemary out of concern for privacy of your friend. Suffice to say that there definitely could be some knowledge gained by reminding the guys with the jane doe file that she could be ruled out on that one. I really appreciate your compassion n this one. We lost a very focused source of inspiration and cohesion for our community when we lost Sally. Many many people here were touched by her beauty and her gracious humor as well as her wit and intellect. She had a laugh that would shake windows. She was a neo-tribalist and she felt that we could all make families that were families of relation and families of affinity. One of the ways that she expressed this viewpoint was through her beautiful maps and diagrams to the faire. She grew up in the San Fernando valley in the same neighborhood in Encino as lots of crazy famous people. We both grew up roaming the same hills and attending the same girl scout camp outs. Things like canoeing on Malibu lake. The colony of artists at Malibu lake still had a sort of influence on the local scene in Agoura, there was an easy mix of the edwardian flair of the sixties with the big looopy craft ethic lettering from the 1920's that I really adored in the early faire signage. I grew up with one of her early signs in my garage, as an Agoura local we sometimes got to salvage old faire signs...the crowds were so big that they ended up having to make hundreds of them to direct the flow of traffic and Sally once told me as a younbg teenager she gained her flowing hand making quite a lot of them. big loopy "F" on "Faire" and an arrow.,
