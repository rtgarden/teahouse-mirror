21 Eric Lethe January 11, 2009 at 04:00PM
Have to tell a bit about this picture...I helped David run the Halberdiers when he played the Sheriff of Chipping-Under-Oakwood while I was attending UCLA. Once graduated, I left for Peace Corps immediately after close of RPFS'81. During my language training in Rabat, Morocco, my group of 60 trainees lived in a spooky deserted "Ecole Superior" for girls where we each had a small cubicle curtained off as our private quarters (in a large dormitory). I got a corner cube, so I had two real walls. I was *terribly* homesick, so I stuck up pictures of my best and closest friends to keep me company, including this picture of David. Now for reasons that may be obvious to all of you, I wasn't too popular amongst my fellow American trainees during those early weeks of Peace Corps. Might have been the fact that I spent most of my time hanging out with our Moroccan instructors...At some point the management of the training cottoned on to the fact that I was learning Moroccan Arabic faster than most of the trainees because I was getting "special lessons" from one especially tall dark and handsome instructor. This was to be seriously discouraged, and management duly reprimanded me and informed me that I was going to be sent home. I was up crying my eyes out in my cube (as loud as you please, since I was friendless amongst these Americans and also because I thought everybody else was out of the building on a language study walk)...when my curtain wall flew open and there stood Steve, a fellow trainee who had run up to the dorms just to grab a notebook, heard me wailing and was concerned, so thought he'd better inquire what was going on. He got one step in the cube and said, "My God! That's a picture of David Springhorn!" Kid you not. That's how widespread the fame of David Springhorn is. And so, mostly because I was a close friend of David's, this guy Steve stopped and listened to my sad tale and then went right out and organized a trainee boycott. He got 90% of them to sign a pledge stating that they would refuse to swear in as Peace Corps Volunteers if I was sent home. Instant solidarity! And all because of this picture and the friendship it represented (and it's a damned good likeness of Ned Courage to boot). Welcome Home, David. It's so good to have you here at the Teahouse.
