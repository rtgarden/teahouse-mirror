On the first Earth Day, I was a 7th grader and decided to support the Earth by walking home from school...28 miles! My poor Mom went looking for me when I didn't come home on the bus and it started to get dark (I did call, but there were no answering machines in those days and no way to leave her a message). I stuck to the normal route for driving to school, so she didn't have any trouble finding me. She was initially mad as hell (because I'd scared her) but very graceful about it once she realized I was just trying to be a good Earth citizen. Yesterday I was in Chicago and spent the day walking and on public transit (commuter train and later the "L"). Trying not to think about the carbon credits I used up getting to Chicago :-(... Here's an eco-trend that's firmly established (along with recycling)...in most business hotels around the world now you get a friendly card telling you that not changing sheets and towels every day supports less use of water and energy resources. I've been using those cards since they arrived. This hotel (Crowne Royal Chicago) actually has a brown paper bag set aside for collecting my recyclable trash! And all the lights in this room are CFL or dimmable spirals. And there are solar panels on the roof! I'm still hoping to live in EcoTopia eventually.
